Housing Near the Cleveland Clinic for Traveling Medical Staff

Cleveland Clinic's main campus sits at East 93rd and Euclid Avenue. Most short-term medical staff prioritize a short, predictable commute over a specific neighborhood. Homes on Cleveland's East and Southeast side put you within roughly a 5-to-20-minute drive, and the HealthLine bus runs directly down Euclid. Rent Finder Cleveland is a local rental team — tell us your assignment location and dates and we'll see what we can help with.

Where the main campus actually is

Cleveland Clinic's main campus is centered near East 93rd Street and Euclid Avenue, just southeast of downtown. If your assignment is at a satellite location — Fairview, Hillcrest, Marymount, or one of the family health centers — the commute math changes, so it helps to confirm your exact building before you fix on an area.

We frame housing here by how you get to work, not by who lives where. What matters for a traveling assignment is a drive or transit route you can count on at shift-change hours, parking that works for your schedule, and a lease term that lines up with your contract.

Thinking in drive times, not neighborhoods

For a 12-week or open-ended assignment, a reliable commute usually beats everything else. Cleveland's East and Southeast side — where most of the homes we work with are concentrated — generally sits within a short drive of the main campus, and the highway grid (I-90, I-77, and I-490) gives you more than one way in when one route backs up.

A few practical things to weigh when you compare options:

  • Off-peak vs. shift-change drive time — a 10-minute midday trip can double at 7 a.m.
  • Whether the home has off-street parking, which matters most for night and rotating shifts
  • Proximity to a highway on-ramp rather than surface streets end to end
  • How close you are to a HealthLine stop if you'd rather not drive daily

Transit: the HealthLine runs to your door

The RTA HealthLine is a bus rapid transit route that runs the length of Euclid Avenue, connecting downtown and Public Square directly to the Cleveland Clinic and University Circle. For staff who would rather skip a car and the parking cost, living within walking distance of a HealthLine stop can turn the commute into a single, predictable ride.

Coverage thins out as you move away from the Euclid corridor, so if car-free living is a priority, tell us up front — it narrows the search in a useful way rather than a limiting one. You can browse the kinds of homes we work with on our Cleveland rentals page to get a feel for what's typical.

How lease terms and vouchers fit a travel assignment

Every home we work with welcomes Housing Choice Vouchers (Section 8) and is kept HUD-inspection-ready. That's worth flagging because some traveling staff — or family members relocating with them — hold a voucher and assume it limits their options near a hospital. It doesn't have to. If that applies to you, our Section 8 housing in Cleveland guide walks through how it works locally.

Be aware of one honest caveat on lease length: standard leases are typically annual, and Cleveland itself has no source-of-income ordinance (only Cleveland Heights, South Euclid, University Heights, Warrensville Heights, and Linndale do). We don't keep a furnished or month-to-month inventory on the shelf, so if your contract is short, tell us the exact dates and we'll be straight with you about what's realistic rather than promising something we can't deliver.

Frequently asked questions

Often yes. The RTA HealthLine bus runs straight down Euclid Avenue to the main campus and University Circle. If car-free living matters to you, tell us and we'll focus the search near a HealthLine stop.
