Sourcing homes for rotating clinicians without a cold search

Travel nurse agencies place the same kind of tenant again and again: a licensed clinician on a defined assignment who needs a clean, move-in-ready home near the facility they were assigned to, often on short notice. The hard part is rarely the nurse — it is re-running a cold rental search in an unfamiliar market for every new contract.

Rent Finder Cleveland is a local rental team. We help people find, tour, and apply for homes we work with across Greater Cleveland. For an agency placing rotating staff, that means one local point of contact who already knows what is opening up, instead of starting from zero on each rotation.

Tell us your team's typical needs once

The more your agency shares up front, the faster we can flag real options for each clinician. Two details matter most for rotating staff: typical assignment length and preferred hospital areas. Share them once for the agency and we will apply them to every placement instead of asking again each time.

  • Typical assignment length (13 weeks, 26 weeks, likely extensions)
  • Which hospital or health system your staff report to
  • Bedrooms and household size (solo clinician vs. traveling with family)
  • Earliest possible move-in date and any hard start deadline
  • Parking, ground-floor, or accessibility needs
  • Budget range and who signs the lease — the agency or the clinician

Where the homes we work with are located

Our current selection is roughly 90+ homes, concentrated on Cleveland's East and Southeast side, with additional homes in some nearby suburbs and in Akron, Lorain, and Elyria. Many sit within a reasonable commute of major employers — including MetroHealth, the Cleveland Clinic main campus, and University Hospitals — which is useful when a clinician needs to be near a specific facility.

We will always tell you honestly whether we have something close to a given hospital right now. If we do not, we will say so rather than push a home that does not fit the commute. You can browse the kind of homes we work with on our houses for rent in Cleveland page.

What we are — and what we are not

We want to be straight about this so your placement team can plan. Rent Finder Cleveland is a local rental team, not a national relocation vendor or a furnished corporate-housing company. The homes we work with are standard residential rentals — generally unfurnished, on standard leases — not turnkey furnished units billed by the night.

Every home we work with also welcomes Housing Choice Vouchers (Section 8) and is kept HUD-inspection-ready, which is a good signal that the homes are maintained to an inspection standard. If your agency's model needs furnished, week-to-week, or all-inclusive housing, we may not be the right fit — but tell us your needs and we will be honest about how we can help.

How a placement moves once you send it

Once we know a clinician's needs, the path is short and there is no fee to the agency for us to source and share options.

  • You send the assignment details, or we use your standing agency preferences on file
  • We reply with homes from our current selection that match the hospital area and timing
  • Your clinician tours in person or virtually — book a showing directly
  • They apply for the home they choose and complete standard tenant screening

Frequently asked questions

Do you provide furnished housing for travel nurses?
Generally no. The homes we work with are standard, usually unfurnished residential rentals on standard leases, not furnished or nightly corporate units. Tell us your agency's needs and we will be honest about whether we can help.
Can you match short 13-week assignments?
Lease terms are set per home, and many are standard longer-term leases. Share your typical assignment length up front so we only flag homes whose terms could realistically fit your clinicians.
Is there a fee for the agency to work with you?
No. There is no fee for us to source and share matching homes. The clinician completes standard tenant application steps with whichever home they choose.
How close are your homes to Cleveland hospitals?
Our current selection sits mostly on the East and Southeast side, with some suburban and Akron/Lorain/Elyria homes; many are a reasonable commute to systems like MetroHealth, Cleveland Clinic, and University Hospitals. We will tell you honestly what is near a given facility right now.
How fast can you tell us what's available?
Usually the same day. Once your agency's preferences are on file, we can quickly match a clinician's hospital area, bedrooms, and timing against what is open now.
