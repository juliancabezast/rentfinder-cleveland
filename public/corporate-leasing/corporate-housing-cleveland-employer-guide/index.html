What 'Corporate Housing' Usually Means

Corporate housing is a catch-all term for rentals set up to make an employee's move simpler than a standard 12-month lease. In practice it can mean a few different things: a fully furnished short-term suite billed to the company, an extended-stay unit near a job site, or an ordinary rental home leased on normal terms while a new hire settles in and decides where to live longer-term.

The right version depends on the assignment. A travel nurse on a 13-week contract needs something furnished and flexible. A household relocating permanently for a role usually just needs a real home to lease while they get their bearings. Knowing which situation you're solving for is the first step, because it changes who you should be talking to.

Corporate Housing Options in Greater Cleveland

Greater Cleveland has steady demand for temporary and relocation housing, driven by anchor employers like the Cleveland Clinic, University Hospitals, Progressive, Sherwin-Williams, and NASA Glenn, plus a large rotation of travel healthcare workers.

That demand is served by a mix of providers: national furnished-suite companies, extended-stay hotels, and — for anyone who needs a straightforward home rather than a hotel-style suite — the regular rental market across the East and Southeast side, several suburbs, and nearby Akron, Lorain, and Elyria.

Rents vary widely by location and bedroom count. HUD publishes Fair Market Rents for the Cleveland-Elyria metro each year, which is a useful reference point when you're budgeting a housing stipend or estimating what a two- or three-bedroom will run. Being Honest About What We Are (and Aren't)

We want to be upfront so no one's time gets wasted. We are a local rental team — not a national relocation vendor, a corporate-suite operator, or a management company. We don't keep a furnished short-term inventory, we don't sell relocation packages, and we don't manage the homes we work with.

What we do have is a concentrated pool of roughly 90 rental homes across Cleveland's East and Southeast side, several suburbs, and Akron, Lorain, and Elyria — every one of which welcomes Housing Choice Vouchers (Section 8) and is kept ready for a HUD inspection. If a relocating employee happens to hold a voucher, that matters; see how it works on our Section 8 housing in Cleveland page.

One local note worth knowing: the city of Cleveland does not have a source-of-income ordinance, though a handful of inner-ring suburbs do — including Cleveland Heights, South Euclid, University Heights, Warrensville Heights, and Linndale. We welcome vouchers everywhere we operate regardless, and we follow the Fair Housing Act in every interaction.
