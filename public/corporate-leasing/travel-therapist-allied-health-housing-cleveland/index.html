Match a home to your assigned facility

Allied-health travelers -- physical therapists (PT), occupational therapists (OT), speech-language pathologists (SLP), plus imaging techs (X-ray, CT, MRI, ultrasound) and lab scientists -- get placed all over Greater Cleveland. A few anchor points help frame the drive:

  • Cleveland Clinic main campus and University Circle facilities (University Hospitals) sit on the East side, near many of the homes we work with.
  • MetroHealth's main campus is on the near West side; ask and we'll be honest about which of our homes make that commute reasonable.
  • Akron-area assignments (Cleveland Clinic Akron General, Summa Health) pair well with our Akron selection.
  • Lorain and Elyria placements (Mercy Health, University Hospitals in Elyria) line up with homes we work with in those cities.

What to send us: your dates and location

The fastest way to get a real answer is a short message with the specifics. The more you include, the sooner we can point you to actual options in our current selection.

  • Assignment start and end dates, or expected length
  • The facility name or address you'll commute to

Honest notes for a 13-week assignment

Most homes we work with lease as standard, unfurnished rentals, and many are set up for longer terms rather than a strict 13-week furnished stay. If a fully furnished, short-term unit is a hard requirement, we'll tell you up front rather than burn your assignment window on a home that can't work.

Every home we work with also welcomes Housing Choice Vouchers (Section 8) and is kept HUD-inspection-ready. Most travelers simply pay market rent, but if a household member uses a voucher, that is no obstacle.

One local detail: Cleveland itself has no source-of-income ordinance, though a handful of inner-ring suburbs (Cleveland Heights, South Euclid, University Heights, Warrensville Heights, and Linndale) do. It rarely affects a market-rate traveler, but we welcome vouchers everywhere we operate and follow the Fair Housing Act in every interaction.
