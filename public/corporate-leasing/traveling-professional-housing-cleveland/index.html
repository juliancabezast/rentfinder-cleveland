What a local rental team can do for a traveling assignment

Rent Finder Cleveland is a local rental team, not a national relocation vendor or corporate-housing company. What we do well is help someone on a temporary assignment find, tour, and apply for a home in the Cleveland area — the same hands-on help we give any renter. If your firm sends consultants or project staff here for multi-month engagements, that local legwork is often the missing piece.

The homes we work with are standard residential rentals — the kind you sign a lease on, not pre-furnished suites billed by the week. That distinction matters for setting expectations, so we'll be direct about what fits your timeline before anyone books a tour. You can browse a sample of what we work with on our Cleveland houses for rent page.

Tell us your assignment length and preferred commute

The two details that tell us the most are how long the assignment runs and where you need to be each day. With those, we can point you toward homes with a realistic drive instead of handing you a long list to sort through yourself.

  • Assignment length, with rough move-in and move-out dates
  • Worksite or client location you'll commute to
  • Bedrooms needed and how many people are staying
  • Parking, ground-floor, or accessibility needs
  • Budget range and whether a Housing Choice Voucher is involved

Where the homes we work with are concentrated

Most of the 90+ homes we work with sit on Cleveland's East and Southeast side, with additional homes in nearby suburbs and in Akron, Lorain, and Elyria. If your worksite is downtown, in the eastern suburbs, or toward Akron, there's a good chance we have something within a reasonable commute.

If your engagement is on the far west side or well outside the county, we'll tell you honestly whether the drive makes sense rather than stretch to fit. Being straight about geography up front saves everyone time. You can see our current areas on the Cleveland rentals overview.

Lease length and furnishings: the honest caveats

Most homes we work with are on standard 12-month leases. Some owners will consider a shorter term, but that is never guaranteed and depends on the specific home — so if your assignment is only a few months, tell us early and we'll be candid about the odds before you get attached to a listing.

We also do not stock furnished or short-term corporate units. If you need weekly billing, a fully furnished place, or built-in relocation services, a dedicated corporate-housing provider is a better fit and we'll say so plainly. What we offer is speed and local knowledge on standard rentals.

Every home we work with welcomes Housing Choice Vouchers (Section 8) and is kept HUD-inspection-ready, and we help voucher and market-rate renters alike. Note that Cleveland itself has no source-of-income ordinance — only a handful of suburbs (Cleveland Heights, South Euclid, University Heights, Warrensville Heights, and Linndale) do — and we follow the Fair Housing Act in every interaction regardless.

Frequently asked questions

Do you offer furnished or short-term corporate housing?
No. The homes we work with are standard unfurnished residential rentals on regular leases, not furnished or weekly corporate suites. If you need a furnished short-stay, a dedicated corporate-housing provider is a better fit.
What is the shortest lease you can find?
Most homes run on 12-month leases. Some owners will consider a shorter term, but it is home-specific and never guaranteed, so tell us your assignment length early and we'll be honest about what's realistic.
Can you help someone who isn't using a voucher?
Yes. Every home we work with welcomes Housing Choice Vouchers, but we help voucher holders and market-rate renters alike. The application steps are the same standard tenant process either way.
How fast can you tell us what's available near a worksite?
Usually the same day. Once we know the assignment length and the commute you're targeting, we can match those against the homes that are open right now.
Is there any cost for a company to reach out?
No. There's no fee to inquire or ask what's available. The renter completes the standard tenant application once they choose a home.
