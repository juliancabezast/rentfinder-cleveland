Corporate Relocation Companies vs. a Local Cleveland Team

National corporate relocation companies handle end-to-end moves — moving trucks, temporary housing, and expense management — across many cities. A local Cleveland rental team like Rent Finder Cleveland is narrower: we help people find, tour, and apply for homes here, and every home we work with welcomes Housing Choice Vouchers. Tell us your team's needs and we'll say honestly whether we're a fit.

What a national relocation company actually does

A national corporate relocation company — sometimes called a relocation management company, or RMC — coordinates the whole move for a transferring employee. That can include booking a van line, arranging temporary or furnished housing, managing the sale or lease-break on the old residence, handling expense reimbursement and tax paperwork, and doing all of it across many U.S. metros under a single contract.

Large employers with frequent, multi-city transfers often need that end-to-end scope. It's a logistics-and-benefits service, and the home search is only one piece of it. The value is breadth: one vendor, many markets, a standardized process.

What a local Cleveland rental team does — and doesn't

Rent Finder Cleveland is a local rental team, not a relocation vendor. We help people find, tour, and apply for homes in the Greater Cleveland area. Every home we work with welcomes Housing Choice Vouchers (Section 8) and is kept HUD-inspection-ready. Our current selection runs to roughly 90+ homes concentrated on Cleveland's East and Southeast side, some suburbs, and Akron, Lorain, and Elyria.

To be clear about what we are not: we don't own or manage these homes, we don't run moving trucks, and we don't hold furnished or short-term inventory. What we do is the on-the-ground home-search step — matching an employee's bedrooms, timing, and location to what's open right now, and getting them in to see it. You can browse a sample on our houses for rent in Cleveland and Section 8 housing pages.

Where a local contact genuinely helps

For the home-search slice of a move, a local team can move faster than a national vendor working the market from out of state.

  • Homes that already welcome Housing Choice Vouchers, so a voucher-holding employee isn't screened out before the search starts
  • Same-week, in-person tours across Greater Cleveland rather than remote listings
  • Familiarity with the CMHA HQS inspection process, a common source of placement delays
  • One point of contact for the local home-search step, so your HR team isn't chasing individual landlords

Where a local team is not the right fit

We'd rather tell you up front when we're not the answer than oversell what we do.

  • You need furnished or short-term corporate housing — we don't hold that kind of inventory
  • You need full-service moving logistics: van lines, storage, or expense management
  • Your employees are relocating to metros we don't cover — we focus on Greater Cleveland plus Akron, Lorain, and Elyria
  • You need a corporate master-lease or centralized invoicing — we're a rental team, not a management company

Source-of-income and Fair Housing rules for Cleveland placements

If any of your employees hold Housing Choice Vouchers, local law matters. The city of Cleveland does not currently have a source-of-income ordinance, so most Cleveland landlords are not required to consider a voucher at all. Several inner-ring suburbs do have one, including Cleveland Heights, South Euclid, University Heights, Warrensville Heights, and Linndale.

That gap is exactly what we close: every home we work with already welcomes vouchers, so a voucher-holding employee isn't turned away before the search even begins. We follow the Fair Housing Act in every interaction — we match on bedroom count, move-in timing, and proximity to a worksite, never on protected characteristics. If a voucher is part of the picture, our Section 8 housing in Cleveland page is a good starting point.
