What to check before renting an older 3-bedroom near Kamm's Corners

Many of the single-family houses in the West Park area predate 1978, which brings them under Cleveland's Codified Ordinances Chapter 365 — the city's Lead-Safe Certification requirement for residential rental units built before January 1, 1978. The certification is valid for two years and follows a visual and dust-wipe evaluation by a certified risk assessor, so it's reasonable to ask a landlord for the current certificate before signing a lease on an older 3-bedroom house.

It's also worth confirming Ohio's security-deposit rules apply the same way here as anywhere else in the state: under Ohio Revised Code 5321.16, a landlord generally must return a deposit within 30 days of move-out with an itemized list of deductions, and owes 5% annual interest on any amount over $50 or one month's rent if the tenant stayed six months or longer. This is general information, not legal advice, so confirm current statute details before signing.
