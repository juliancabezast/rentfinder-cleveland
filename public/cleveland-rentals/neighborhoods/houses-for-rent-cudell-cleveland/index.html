Renting a house in Cudell, Cleveland

Cudell is a near-west-side Cleveland neighborhood that runs along the Lake Erie shoreline, bordered by the Detroit-Shoreway district (home to the Gordon Square Arts District) to the east and Edgewater to the west. The neighborhood sits within ZIP code 44102 and developed largely in the early 1900s, so the housing stock is a mix of frame single-family homes and two-family duplexes on compact city lots.

How much does it cost to rent a house in Cudell?

Cudell doesn't have its own published rent index, so the closest grounded benchmark is citywide Cleveland data. Getting around Cudell

Cudell is served by the GCRTA Red Line's West Boulevard-Cudell rapid station along Madison Avenue, giving direct rail access to downtown Cleveland (Tower City) in one direction and Cleveland Hopkins International Airport in the other, per GCRTA's published rail network. Several bus routes also run along Detroit Avenue and Madison Avenue connecting to downtown and neighboring west-side suburbs, and the Shoreway (I-90) provides quick highway access along the lakefront.

  • ZIP code: 44102, shared with the neighboring Detroit-Shoreway district
  • Transit: GCRTA Red Line (West Boulevard-Cudell station) plus Detroit Ave and Madison Ave bus routes
  • Highways: Quick access to I-90 (Shoreway) along the lakefront
  • Nearby: Cudell Commons Park on Lake Erie; Gordon Square Arts District just to the east

What kind of housing is available in Cudell?

Housing in Cudell is a mix of early-1900s single-family frame homes and two-family duplexes, typical of Cleveland's near-west-side lakefront neighborhoods. Lot sizes are modest, and many homes have small front porches and basements. Across our own Cleveland portfolio, roughly half of the units are duplexes rather than standalone houses, so renters searching Cudell listings should expect both formats. Section 8 and voucher-friendly rentals in Cudell

Every home Rent Finder Cleveland manages — including homes in the 44102 area — accepts Housing Choice Vouchers (Section 8) and is HUD-inspection-ready, so voucher holders can tour and apply with confidence. Cuyahoga Metropolitan Housing Authority (CMHA) administers the local voucher program and accepts preliminary applications year-round through its online applicant portal; selection then runs through periodic lotteries rather than strict first-come-first-served order.

One important honest note: Ohio has no statewide law requiring landlords to accept vouchers, and the City of Cleveland does not currently have a source-of-income protection ordinance, so acceptance still depends on the individual landlord.
