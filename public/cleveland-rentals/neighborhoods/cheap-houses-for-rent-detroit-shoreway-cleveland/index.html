Affordable houses in Detroit-Shoreway

Detroit-Shoreway sits in ZIP code 44102 on Cleveland's near-west side, between Ohio City and the Lake Erie shore. Much of its housing dates from the early 1900s: modest wood-frame single-family houses and two-family duplexes, many with small front yards and full basements — older housing stock that tends to rent below newer, larger apartment developments elsewhere in the city.

Getting around Detroit-Shoreway

Detroit Avenue is the neighborhood's commercial spine, running past the Gordon Square Arts District's theaters, restaurants, and shops. RTA bus routes run the length of Detroit Avenue and Lorain Avenue, connecting to Downtown and to the Red Line at the Ohio City station just to the east. Edgewater Park, part of the Cleveland Metroparks Lakefront system, is a short distance to the north along the lake.

  • ZIP code: 44102 (shared with Cudell)
  • Housing stock: Early-1900s single-family homes and duplexes
  • Transit: RTA bus routes on Detroit and Lorain Avenues; near the Ohio City Red Line station
  • Nearby park: Edgewater Park (Cleveland Metroparks Lakefront)

Section 8 and vouchers in Detroit-Shoreway

Every rental home we manage in and around Detroit-Shoreway accepts Housing Choice Vouchers (Section 8) and is HUD-inspection-ready — a useful fact for voucher holders trying to find an affordable house in this ZIP code. Keep in mind that Ohio has no statewide source-of-income protection law and Cleveland has not enacted a citywide voucher-protection ordinance, so acceptance policies vary outside of the homes we manage. See our Cleveland Section 8 guide for the local program details.

For renters using a Housing Choice Voucher, the Cuyahoga Metropolitan Housing Authority (CMHA) sets an income limit and a payment standard that determines how much of the rent the voucher covers; check the current figures directly on CMHA's site before budgeting for a specific unit. Because a lower asking rent generally means a smaller gap between the payment standard and the unit's rent, the smaller homes common in Detroit-Shoreway can be a practical starting point for a voucher search.

What to expect on a budget in this neighborhood

Affordable houses in Detroit-Shoreway are typically older construction, so it's worth asking about the age of major systems (roof, furnace, water heater) and whether utilities are included in the quoted rent, since older homes vary widely in efficiency. Basements are common and often provide extra storage or laundry hookups even in smaller units. Because the neighborhood mixes single-family houses with duplexes, a "cheap house" search here often turns up a full-floor duplex unit at a similar price point to a smaller stand-alone house — worth comparing both when weighing space against monthly cost.
