Renting a house in Little Italy

Little Italy is one of Cleveland's smallest and most distinctive residential districts, centered on Mayfield Road within the city's University-area neighborhoods, roughly five miles east of Downtown Cleveland. The district sits directly against Lake View Cemetery to the east and blends into University Circle to the south, putting renters within walking distance of Case Western Reserve University, the Cleveland Museum of Art, and Severance Music Center.

Housing stock here is dense and older — largely narrow-lot single-family and two-family homes built in the early 1900s along streets like Coltman Road, Random Road, and Cornell Road, with small yards and on-street parking typical of the era. Because the footprint of Little Italy is small, the number of standalone houses for rent at any given time is limited, and renters searching this specific pocket of the city often expand into the surrounding University Circle rental market, which has a larger supply of both houses and apartments.

How much does it cost to rent a house near Little Italy?

There is no rent index published specifically for a district as small as Little Italy, so the most reliable reference point is Cleveland's citywide rent data by bedroom count. Per Zumper's rent report dated July 2026, the citywide median for a 2-bedroom home is about $1,100 a month and about $1,350 for a 3-bedroom, up 4.2% year-over-year. Homes close to University Circle employers and institutions tend to price toward the higher end of these citywide ranges rather than the lower end.

For a second reference point, RentCafe's report dated July 2026 on professionally managed Cleveland apartments puts the citywide average at $1,564 a month for a unit averaging 787 square feet. That figure skews higher because it reflects newer, professionally managed buildings rather than the older, individually owned houses more typical of a district like Little Italy — treat it as a ceiling rather than a typical price for this kind of home.

Getting around Little Italy and University Circle

Little Italy benefits from some of the best transit access on Cleveland's East Side. GCRTA's Red Line heavy rail connects the University Circle station directly to Downtown's Tower City and on to Cleveland Hopkins International Airport, while the HealthLine bus rapid transit runs along Euclid Avenue between Public Square and University Circle in a dedicated median busway. Together, these two lines make it realistic to live in Little Italy without a personal vehicle for many Downtown or University Circle commutes.

  • Transit: Red Line heavy rail + HealthLine BRT serve University Circle
  • Nearby institutions: Case Western Reserve University, Cleveland Museum of Art, Severance Music Center
  • Green space: Lake View Cemetery borders the neighborhood
  • Distance: About 5 miles east of Downtown Cleveland

Little Italy's landmarks and everyday life

Beyond housing, Little Italy is anchored by the small business district along Mayfield Road, with restaurants, cafes, and shops within a few blocks of most homes in the area. Lake View Cemetery, one of the city's largest historic cemeteries, borders the neighborhood and offers walking paths open to the public.
