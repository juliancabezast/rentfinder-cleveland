Finding a cheap house to rent in Glenville

Glenville sits in ZIP code 44108 on Cleveland's northeast side, bordered by University Circle to the south and crossed by St. Clair Avenue's commercial strip. Its housing stock is dominated by early-1900s two- and three-story homes, many split into duplex units, which keeps a good share of listings priced below what newer apartment buildings charge elsewhere in the city.

What a budget rental looks like in Glenville

On the lower end of the market, one-bedroom units — mostly upper floors of converted duplexes — can be found from roughly $700 a month, with two-bedroom homes generally starting around $800. Three-bedroom houses run higher, typically $1,050 to $1,300, but even that upper range sits close to Cleveland's citywide median rather than above it.

Zumper's July 4, 2026 report puts Cleveland's overall median asking rent at $1,250/month, with 2-bedrooms at $1,100 and 3-bedrooms at $1,350 — Glenville's budget-tier listings generally price at or below those citywide figures. RentCafe's July 2, 2026 report on larger, professionally-managed apartment buildings shows a much higher citywide average of $1,818 for a 2-bedroom, which reflects a different segment of the market than Glenville's individually-owned rental houses.

Stretching your rental budget in Glenville

Because most Glenville rentals are older, individually-owned homes rather than large managed complexes, whether utilities are included in the rent can shift the real monthly cost significantly — always confirm this before comparing two listings on price alone. Some units also include off-street parking or a garage, which can offset the cost of maintaining a car in a neighborhood well served by transit.

RTA bus routes run along St. Clair Avenue and Superior Avenue, both connecting to the HealthLine bus rapid transit corridor on Euclid Avenue, which links Public Square downtown to University Circle without a car. Rockefeller Park and its Cultural Gardens run along the neighborhood's western edge, offering free outdoor space close to home.

It's also worth asking each landlord directly about lease length and move-in costs — a lower monthly rent can still come with a security deposit and last month's rent due upfront, so the total move-in cost matters as much as the advertised monthly figure when comparing budget listings against each other.

Section 8 and voucher-friendly homes in Glenville

A Housing Choice Voucher can make an already affordable Glenville rental even more manageable — every home we work with in the area accepts Section 8 and is HUD-inspection-ready. Ohio has no statewide source-of-income protection law, and the City of Cleveland does not currently require landlords to accept vouchers; only Cleveland Heights, South Euclid, University Heights, Warrensville Heights, and Linndale have local ordinances doing so.
