Renting near Downtown Cleveland

Downtown Cleveland is the city's central business district, built around Public Square and the Terminal Tower/Tower City complex, with the Warehouse District and Gateway sports district nearby. It's Cleveland's employment and entertainment core, and its residential stock skews heavily toward apartments and converted lofts rather than detached houses.

Because Downtown itself has very little standalone-house inventory, renters specifically searching for houses for rent in Cleveland near Downtown are often better served by nearby, more residential neighborhoods. How much does it cost to rent Downtown?

Downtown's housing mix (apartments and lofts) tends to run at or above Cleveland's citywide averages. Per RentCafe (Yardi), reporting July 2, 2026, the citywide average rent across professionally managed apartments — the category most Downtown units fall into — is $1,564/month for 787 sq. ft. (up 1.66% year-over-year), with 2-bedroom units averaging $1,818 and 3-bedroom units averaging $2,716. Zumper's July 4, 2026 report, which reflects a broader median across all listing types citywide, puts the overall median lower, at $1,250/month.

For comparison, Rent Finder Cleveland's own selection of Section 8-ready rental homes across Greater Cleveland — mostly houses, duplexes, and small multi-family buildings outside the immediate Downtown core — typically rents from $700 to $1,800 a month, with most homes around $1,000.

Getting around Downtown

Downtown Cleveland is the region's transit hub. Tower City is where all four GCRTA rail lines converge: the Red Line (heavy rail to Cleveland Hopkins Airport and the East Side, including University Circle and Windermere), the Blue and Green Lines (light rail to Shaker Heights), and the Waterfront Line (light rail to the Flats and North Coast lakefront attractions). The HealthLine bus rapid transit route also starts near Public Square and runs the length of Euclid Avenue to University Circle and East Cleveland.

  • Landmarks: Public Square, Terminal Tower/Tower City, Warehouse District, Gateway
  • Transit hub: Tower City — Red, Blue, Green, and Waterfront rail lines all converge here
  • Bus rapid transit: HealthLine along Euclid Avenue to University Circle
  • Housing type: Mostly apartments and lofts; standalone rental houses are limited

Section 8 and voucher-friendly rentals near Downtown

Renters using a Housing Choice Voucher (Section 8) apply through the Cuyahoga Metropolitan Housing Authority (CMHA), which administers the program countywide via a year-round preliminary application and a mini-lottery selection process — see CMHA's voucher portal for details. Note that Ohio has no statewide source-of-income law, and the City of Cleveland (Downtown included) has not enacted a local ordinance requiring landlords to accept vouchers as of this writing; a handful of Cuyahoga County suburbs have their own protections instead.

Frequently asked questions

Are there many houses for rent in Downtown Cleveland?
Not many — Downtown's housing stock is dominated by apartments and converted lofts rather than standalone houses. Renters specifically wanting a house typically have more options in Cleveland's residential neighborhoods just outside the Downtown core.
How much does it cost to rent Downtown?
Downtown rents track close to Cleveland's citywide apartment averages: RentCafe (July 2026) reports a citywide average of $1,564/month for professionally managed apartments, while Zumper's broader citywide median (July 2026) is $1,250/month. Actual Downtown units, being mostly apartments, tend toward the higher end.
What's the transit hub for Downtown Cleveland?
Tower City, adjacent to Public Square, is where all four GCRTA rail lines — Red, Blue, Green, and Waterfront — converge. The HealthLine bus rapid transit route also connects Downtown to University Circle along Euclid Avenue.

Can Section 8 voucher holders rent in Downtown Cleveland?
