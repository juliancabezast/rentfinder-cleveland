Renting a house in North Collinwood

North Collinwood is Cleveland's lakefront neighborhood on the far northeast side, largely within ZIP code 44110, running along Lake Erie north of St. Clair Avenue. It's best known for the Waterloo Arts District, a stretch of Waterloo Road lined with galleries, music venues, and studios. Housing stock is mostly early-1900s bungalows, Cape Cods, and two-family duplexes on modest lots.

Many of the residential blocks closest to the lake feature small single-family homes originally built for workers at the rail yards and lakefront industry that shaped this part of the city. Farther inland toward Waterloo Road, the mix shifts toward two-family duplexes, several of which sit within walking distance of the arts district's galleries and coffee shops.

How much does it cost to rent in North Collinwood?

Two-bedroom homes in North Collinwood typically rent from about $750 to $1,000 a month, and three-bedroom homes run roughly $950 to $1,200. These ranges sit within the broader $700–$1,800 span across the East Side homes Rent Finder Cleveland manages, where the typical rent is close to $1,000. Citywide, Cleveland's median asking rent was $1,250 a month as of July 2026, per Zumper, with three-bedroom units averaging $1,350 — North Collinwood's rental stock generally prices below both figures.

Getting around North Collinwood

North Collinwood's lakefront location and arts district give it a distinct character within Cleveland's East Side.

  • ZIP code: 44110, on Cleveland's far northeast lakefront
  • Transit: GCRTA bus routes along Waterloo Road and St. Clair Avenue; I-90 runs along the neighborhood's southern edge for quick access downtown or toward the Lake County suburbs
  • Arts & culture: The Waterloo Arts District, with monthly gallery walks along Waterloo Road
  • Geography: Directly on Lake Erie, making it one of the city's northernmost residential neighborhoods
  • Nearby suburbs: Borders Euclid to the east, a short drive along St. Clair Avenue or I-90

Section 8 and vouchers in North Collinwood

Every home we manage in North Collinwood accepts Housing Choice Vouchers (Section 8) and is HUD-inspection-ready. CMHA, the Cuyahoga Metropolitan Housing Authority, runs voucher applications year-round with no fixed closing date. As with the rest of Cleveland, there's no city ordinance protecting voucher holders from source-of-income discrimination, so ask directly whether a specific landlord accepts vouchers. North Collinwood vs. South Collinwood

North Collinwood sits directly on Lake Erie, while South Collinwood is the inland portion of the same broader Collinwood district, centered on the historic Nottingham area farther from the shoreline. Both fall largely within ZIP 44110, and Rent Finder Cleveland manages voucher-friendly homes in both.
