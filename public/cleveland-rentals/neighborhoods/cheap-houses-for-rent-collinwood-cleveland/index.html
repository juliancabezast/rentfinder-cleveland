Cheap Houses for Rent in Collinwood, Cleveland

Collinwood, in Cleveland's ZIP code 44110, is one of the city's lower-cost neighborhoods for a rental house. Where to find a cheap house to rent in Collinwood

The most budget-friendly rental houses in Collinwood sit within ZIP code 44110, on Cleveland's far northeast side, split between the inland Collinwood-Nottingham blocks and the North Shore Collinwood area closer to Lake Erie. Because the housing stock is largely early-1900s single-family homes and duplexes rather than newer construction, asking rents here tend to run below what larger, professionally-managed apartment buildings charge citywide.

How cheap is 'cheap' in Collinwood?

On the lower end, two-bedroom houses and duplex units in 44110 can be found from about $750 a month, with most falling somewhere between $750 and $1,000 depending on condition, size, and whether utilities are included in the rent. Three-bedroom homes stretch a bit higher, generally $1,000 to $1,300, but even the top of that range sits below Cleveland's overall median asking rent.

For context, Zumper's July 4, 2026 report puts Cleveland's citywide median at $1,250/month, with 2-bedrooms averaging $1,100 and 3-bedrooms averaging $1,350 — Collinwood's budget-tier houses generally undercut both figures. RentCafe's July 2, 2026 report on larger managed apartment buildings shows an even higher citywide average of $1,818 for a 2-bedroom unit, which reflects newer complexes rather than the older individually-owned houses that make up most of Collinwood's rental stock.

What keeps rent low in this part of Cleveland

Collinwood's housing stock is dominated by older, individually-owned single-family homes and duplexes rather than large apartment complexes, which is the main reason prices trend below citywide averages. Renters looking to stay on the lower end of the range should ask upfront whether heat, water, and electric are included, since an all-in monthly cost can shift the real comparison between two listings that look similar on paper.

The neighborhood is served by RTA bus routes into downtown and sits near the Red Line's Windermere station, the eastern end of the heavy-rail line that also reaches Cleveland Hopkins Airport, which can reduce the need for a car and its associated costs. Waterloo Road's Waterloo Arts District puts a commercial strip for everyday errands within a few blocks of many Collinwood addresses, and I-90 (the Lakeland Freeway) is a short drive from most Collinwood streets for renters who do drive.

  • ZIP code: 44110
  • Transit: RTA bus lines and the Red Line's Windermere terminus nearby
  • Commercial corridor: Waterloo Road / Waterloo Arts District
  • Highway access: I-90 (Lakeland Freeway) a short drive from most streets

Section 8 and voucher-friendly homes in Collinwood

Every home we manage in Collinwood accepts Housing Choice Vouchers (Section 8) and is HUD-inspection-ready, which can make a voucher a useful tool for keeping your monthly out-of-pocket rent low. Keep in mind that Ohio has no statewide source-of-income protection law, and the City of Cleveland does not currently require landlords to accept vouchers — only a handful of Cuyahoga County suburbs, including Cleveland Heights, South Euclid, University Heights, Warrensville Heights, and Linndale, have local ordinances doing so. Apply for a voucher through CMHA, and see our Cleveland Section 8 guide for the full application process.
