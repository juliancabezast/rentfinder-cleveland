Renting a house in South Collinwood

South Collinwood is the inland half of Cleveland's larger Collinwood district, historically known as Nottingham, sitting farther from Lake Erie than its northern counterpart. It falls within ZIP code 44110 on the city's far northeast side. Housing stock is dominated by early-1900s worker-cottage bungalows and two-family duplexes built near the rail and industrial corridors that once employed the neighborhood.

Streets in South Collinwood tend to be quieter and set back from the retail strips along St. Clair Avenue, with modest lot sizes and detached garages common on the older bungalow-style housing. Because the area developed around rail and manufacturing jobs rather than lakefront tourism, the housing stock here reads as more uniformly residential than the blocks closer to the Waterloo Arts District to the north.

How much does it cost to rent in South Collinwood?

Two-bedroom homes in South Collinwood typically rent from about $750 to $1,000 a month, and three-bedroom homes run roughly $950 to $1,150. Citywide, Cleveland's median asking rent was $1,250 a month as of July 2026, per Zumper, with two-bedroom units averaging $1,100 — South Collinwood's inland housing stock generally prices below that citywide figure across most bedroom sizes.

Getting around South Collinwood

South Collinwood's inland location still keeps it within easy reach of I-90 and downtown.

  • ZIP code: 44110, the inland portion of Cleveland's Collinwood district
  • Transit: GCRTA bus routes along St. Clair Avenue and the E. 152nd/E. 156th Street corridors; I-90 is a short drive north for access downtown or toward Lake County
  • History: Historically known as Nottingham, once tied to nearby rail yards and industry
  • Nearby: Borders North Collinwood and the Waterloo Arts District to the north
  • Neighboring suburb: Euclid lies just to the east, along the St. Clair Avenue corridor

South Collinwood vs. North Collinwood

If you'd prefer to live directly on Lake Erie, North Collinwood sits along the shoreline and includes the Waterloo Arts District, while South Collinwood is set back from the lake around the historic Nottingham area. Both are largely within ZIP 44110.
