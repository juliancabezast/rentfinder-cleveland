Renting a house in St. Clair-Superior

St. Clair-Superior sits on Cleveland's near-east side, just north of downtown, in ZIP code 44103, and takes its name from the two parallel avenues — St. Clair Avenue and Superior Avenue — that run through it. The neighborhood is also home to AsiaTown, a business district of restaurants and shops along Payne Avenue. Housing stock is a mix of early-1900s two- and three-family homes, brick duplexes, and smaller single-family houses, many built close to the street on narrow lots typical of Cleveland's older near-east-side blocks.

The neighborhood's proximity to downtown and the Campus District makes it a common option for renters who want a shorter commute without downtown apartment pricing. Many blocks mix owner-occupied homes with rentals, and porches, small front yards, and detached garages are common features on the older housing stock here.

How much does it cost to rent in St. Clair-Superior?

Two-bedroom homes in the 44103 area typically rent from about $750 to $1,050 a month, and three-bedroom homes commonly run $1,000 to $1,350, depending on size, condition, and whether utilities are included. These figures sit within the broader $700–$1,800 range across the East Side homes Rent Finder Cleveland manages, where the typical rent is close to $1,000. Citywide, Cleveland's median asking rent was $1,250 a month as of July 2026, per Zumper — St. Clair-Superior's near-east-side rental stock tends to price at or below that citywide figure.

Getting around St. Clair-Superior

St. Clair-Superior is well served by GCRTA bus routes and sits close to the highways that connect downtown to the rest of the East Side.

  • ZIP code: 44103, shared with parts of the Hough neighborhood
  • Transit: GCRTA bus routes along St. Clair Avenue and Superior Avenue; the HealthLine bus-rapid-transit runs along Euclid Avenue just south of the neighborhood, connecting Public Square to University Circle
  • Highways: Close to I-90 and I-77 for driving downtown or to the East Side suburbs
  • Local landmarks: The AsiaTown business district along Payne Avenue; proximity to Downtown Cleveland and the Campus District

Section 8 and vouchers in St. Clair-Superior

Every home Rent Finder Cleveland manages — including our St. Clair-Superior-area rentals — accepts Housing Choice Vouchers (Section 8) and is HUD-inspection-ready. Cuyahoga Metropolitan Housing Authority (CMHA) administers the voucher program locally and accepts preliminary applications year-round. Ohio has no statewide law requiring landlords to accept vouchers, and the City of Cleveland itself does not have a source-of-income protection ordinance — so always confirm directly with any specific landlord.
