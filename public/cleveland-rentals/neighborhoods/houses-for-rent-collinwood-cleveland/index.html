Renting a house in Collinwood

Collinwood sits on Cleveland's far northeast side, largely within ZIP code 44110, split between the North Shore Collinwood lakefront area and the inland Collinwood-Nottingham section. It's one of the neighborhoods where our current selection has its strongest presence, with more rental homes here than in almost any other Cleveland ZIP code we serve.

Housing here is a mix of early-1900s single-family homes and duplexes, many with front porches and small yards typical of Cleveland's older streetcar-era neighborhoods. Streets running north from St. Clair Avenue and Ivanhoe Road toward the lakefront tend to have larger, older homes, while blocks closer to Waterloo Road mix residential and small commercial buildings.

How much is rent in Collinwood?

Two-bedroom houses in the 44110 area generally rent from about $750 to $1,000 a month, and three-bedroom homes commonly run from roughly $1,000 to $1,300, depending on condition, size, and whether utilities are included. Collinwood tends to price at or slightly below Cleveland's overall median asking rent, which Zumper's July 4, 2026 report put at $1,250/mo citywide.

RentCafe's July 2, 2026 report, which measures larger professionally-managed apartment buildings rather than individually owned rental houses, shows a citywide average of $1,818 for a 2-bedroom apartment and $2,716 for a 3-bedroom. Collinwood's older rental houses and duplexes generally cost noticeably less than those newer managed-apartment averages.

Getting around and what's nearby

Collinwood is served by RTA bus routes connecting to downtown and the Waterloo Arts District along Waterloo Road, one of the neighborhood's commercial and cultural corridors. The Red Line's Windermere station, the eastern terminus of the heavy-rail line connecting Cleveland Hopkins Airport to downtown and the east side, sits at the edge of the neighborhood.

Drivers can reach I-90 (Lakeland Freeway) within a few minutes from most of Collinwood, and downtown Cleveland is roughly a 15-minute drive southwest. The North Shore Collinwood section along the Lake Erie bluff offers additional green space beyond the neighborhood's residential streets.

  • Transit: Multiple RTA bus lines; near the Red Line's Windermere terminus
  • Arts/commercial: Waterloo Arts District along Waterloo Road
  • Lakefront: North Shore Collinwood section borders Lake Erie

Section 8 and vouchers in Collinwood

Collinwood is one of our largest concentrations of Section 8-ready rental homes — every unit we work with there accepts Housing Choice Vouchers and is HUD-inspection-ready. Keep in mind Ohio has no statewide source-of-income protection law, and the City of Cleveland does not currently require landlords to accept vouchers; only Cleveland Heights, South Euclid, University Heights, Warrensville Heights, and Linndale have local ordinances protecting voucher holders. Apply for a voucher through CMHA, and see our Cleveland Section 8 guide for details on the process.
