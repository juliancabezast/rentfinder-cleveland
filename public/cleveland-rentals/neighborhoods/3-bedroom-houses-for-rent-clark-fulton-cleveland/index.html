3-bedroom houses in Clark-Fulton

Clark-Fulton is a near-west side Cleveland neighborhood centered on the West 25th Street and Clark Avenue corridor, directly adjacent to the MetroHealth main campus, between Ohio City to the north and Stockyards to the south. The neighborhood's housing stock dates largely to the late 1800s and early 1900s and consists mostly of single-family homes and duplexes built close together on a dense street grid.

A 3-bedroom rental in this part of Cleveland is typically one of the larger single-family houses on the block rather than a smaller duplex unit, since older Clark-Fulton duplexes tend to split into one- and two-bedroom halves. How much does a 3-bedroom house cost in Clark-Fulton?

There's no rent index specific to Clark-Fulton, so citywide Cleveland data is the closest available benchmark for a 3-bedroom home here. Zumper's July 4, 2026 report puts Cleveland's median 3-bedroom asking rent at $1,350/month, while RentCafe's July 2, 2026 report on professionally-managed apartment buildings shows a much higher citywide average of $2,716 for a 3-bedroom unit — a gap that reflects newer, larger buildings rather than the older single-family housing typical of Clark-Fulton.

What a 3-bedroom home in Clark-Fulton typically includes

Most 3-bedroom houses in Clark-Fulton are late-1800s to early-1900s two-story homes with a basement and a small yard, built close to their neighbors on a tight urban grid rather than set on larger suburban-style lots. Because these are individually owned properties, finishes and included features vary — some have updated kitchens or laundry hookups, others retain more original layouts, so confirming specifics at a showing is worth the trip.

The neighborhood's proximity to MetroHealth's main campus makes it a common search for hospital staff and visiting family members who want a short commute to the medical center.

Transit and getting around from Clark-Fulton

Clark-Fulton is served by GCRTA bus routes along West 25th Street and Clark Avenue, connecting directly to downtown and to the MetroHealth main campus, per RTA's published route network. The neighborhood also sits close to I-90 and I-71, making it a practical location for drivers heading downtown or toward the west-side suburbs.
