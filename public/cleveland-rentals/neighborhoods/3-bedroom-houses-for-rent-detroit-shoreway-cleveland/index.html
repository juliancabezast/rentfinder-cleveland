3-bedroom houses in Detroit-Shoreway

Detroit-Shoreway is a near-west-side Cleveland neighborhood in ZIP code 44102, running along Detroit Avenue between Ohio City and the Lake Erie shoreline. Its best-known feature is the Gordon Square Arts District, a strip of restored early-1900s storefronts, theaters, and restaurants along Detroit Avenue, and the neighborhood sits just inland from Edgewater Park on the west-side lakefront.

The housing stock is mostly early-20th-century construction — single-family houses and duplexes with front porches and small lots, similar to much of Cleveland's near-west side. How much does a 3-bedroom house rent for here?

Citywide, Zumper's rent report dated July 4, 2026 puts Cleveland's median 3-bedroom asking rent at $1,350 a month. A separate figure from RentCafe (Yardi), dated July 2, 2026, shows a much higher $2,716 average for 3-bedroom units — but that number reflects larger, professionally managed apartment complexes (roughly 1,483 sq. ft. on average), not the smaller houses and duplexes typical of near-west-side neighborhoods like Detroit-Shoreway. The two figures aren't directly comparable; use Zumper's median as the closer proxy for a stand-alone rental house.

Within our current selection, currently available 3-bedroom homes across Cleveland range from about $1,100 to $1,500 a month, averaging roughly $1,250 — broadly in line with the citywide Zumper figure.

What's nearby in Detroit-Shoreway

The Gordon Square Arts District anchors the neighborhood's commercial life along Detroit Avenue, with independent theaters, restaurants, and small shops. Edgewater Park, part of the Cleveland Metroparks Lakefront system, sits just to the west along Lake Erie. Multiple RTA bus routes run along Detroit Avenue and connect the neighborhood to Downtown and Ohio City, where the RTA Red Line has a station.

  • ZIP code: 44102 (shared with Cudell)
  • Landmark: Gordon Square Arts District along Detroit Avenue
  • Lakefront: Edgewater Park nearby
  • Transit: RTA bus routes along Detroit Avenue; near the Red Line at Ohio City

What to expect in a 3-bedroom rental here

A 3-bedroom in Detroit-Shoreway is more often a full duplex unit or a stand-alone house than a large apartment, given the neighborhood's early-1900s building stock. Expect a full basement in most cases, useful for storage or laundry hookups, and a small-to-modest yard on most lots. Because homes in this ZIP code vary in age and condition, it's worth confirming which utilities are included in the rent and the condition of major systems (roof, furnace, water heater) before signing a lease — details that can shift the effective monthly cost more than the base rent figure alone.

Section 8 vouchers for 3-bedroom homes

Every home we work with — including 3-bedroom houses in and around Detroit-Shoreway — accepts Housing Choice Vouchers and is HUD-inspection-ready. That said, Ohio has no statewide source-of-income protection law, and Cleveland proper has not adopted a citywide ordinance requiring landlords to accept vouchers, so acceptance policies vary by individual property owner. Read more in our Cleveland Section 8 guide.

For voucher holders, the Cuyahoga Metropolitan Housing Authority (CMHA) sets a payment standard by bedroom size that determines how much of a 3-bedroom home's rent the voucher can cover; check CMHA's current chart directly rather than relying on a fixed number, since these figures are updated periodically.
