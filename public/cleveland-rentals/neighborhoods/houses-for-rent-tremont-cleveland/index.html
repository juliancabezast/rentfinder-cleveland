Getting around Tremont

Tremont borders Ohio City to the north and Clark-Fulton, home to the MetroHealth main campus, to the west. The neighborhood is served by RTA bus routes connecting to Downtown Cleveland and nearby near-west-side neighborhoods, and it sits close to I-490 and I-71 for drivers heading toward Downtown or the southern suburbs.

Section 8 and housing vouchers in Tremont

Renters using a Housing Choice Voucher (Section 8) apply through the Cuyahoga Metropolitan Housing Authority (CMHA), which runs the program for Cuyahoga County. CMHA accepts preliminary applications year-round via its online voucher portal, and applicants are selected through periodic mini-lotteries rather than a strict waiting-list order.

It's worth being clear-eyed here: Ohio has no statewide law requiring landlords to accept vouchers, and the City of Cleveland — Tremont included — has not passed a local source-of-income ordinance as of this writing (a small set of Cuyahoga County suburbs, like Cleveland Heights, have their own protections). Independent of what local law requires, every Rent Finder Cleveland home, including those in and around Tremont, welcomes Section 8 vouchers and is kept HUD-inspection-ready.
