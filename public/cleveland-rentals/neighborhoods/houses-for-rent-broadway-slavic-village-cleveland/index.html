Renting a house in Broadway-Slavic Village

Broadway-Slavic Village is the official City Planning Commission name for the historic core of the Slavic Village neighborhood, centered on the Broadway Avenue business corridor on Cleveland's southeast side. The area was historically settled by Polish and Czech immigrant communities, and much of its housing stock reflects that early-20th-century development pattern of modest single-family homes and duplexes.

What does it cost to rent a house here?

Cleveland-wide, three-bedroom homes are running around $1,350 a month and two-bedroom homes around $1,100, per Zumper's July 2026 rent report. Our own selection across Cleveland spans roughly $700 to $1,800 a month, with a typical rent near $1,000 — and homes in ZIP 44105, which covers Broadway-Slavic Village and neighboring Union-Miles, tend to fall toward the more affordable end of that range.

Transit, streets, and what's nearby

The neighborhood sits along Broadway Avenue, a historic commercial corridor, with additional shopping along Fleet Avenue. Multiple RTA bus routes connect the area to downtown Cleveland, and Interstate 77 and Interstate 490 both run nearby, making it straightforward to reach downtown or the southern suburbs by car.

  • Side of city: Southeast
  • Highways: I-77 and I-490 nearby

Section 8 and Housing Choice Vouchers here

Every home Rent Finder Cleveland works with — in this neighborhood and across our available homes — accepts Section 8 and is HUD-inspection-ready. Voucher holders apply through the Cuyahoga Metropolitan Housing Authority (CMHA); our Section 8 housing guide walks through the application, waitlist, and inspection process.

Keep in mind that Ohio has no statewide source-of-income protection law, and Cleveland itself does not currently have a voucher-nondiscrimination ordinance as of July 2026, so always confirm a landlord's policy directly.
