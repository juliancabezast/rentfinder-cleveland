Renting a house near Stockyards

Stockyards is a near-west side Cleveland neighborhood adjacent to Clark-Fulton, taking its name from the livestock stockyards historically located in the area. The surrounding blocks are made up largely of early-1900s single-family homes and duplexes on narrow lots typical of Cleveland's west side, mixed with light-industrial buildings that reflect the neighborhood's stockyard-era history.

We're upfront that our own rental listings aren't concentrated in Stockyards specifically. Getting around and what's nearby

Stockyards is adjacent to Clark-Fulton, the near-west side corridor along Clark Avenue and W. 25th Street that includes the MetroHealth main campus. RTA bus routes run along Clark Avenue and connect toward downtown Cleveland, and the neighborhood is a short drive from I-71.

Downtown Cleveland is roughly a 10-minute drive northeast via I-71 or Clark Avenue, and the neighborhood's proximity to MetroHealth's main campus makes it a common stop for RTA routes serving hospital shifts and appointments.

  • Location: Near-west side, directly adjacent to Clark-Fulton
  • Transit: RTA bus routes along Clark Avenue toward downtown
  • Nearby institutions: MetroHealth main campus, just north in Clark-Fulton
  • Roads: Close to I-71

Section 8 and vouchers near Stockyards

Every home we work with anywhere in Greater Cleveland accepts Section 8 / Housing Choice Vouchers and is HUD-inspection-ready, though we don't currently have listings we can point to directly in Stockyards. Ohio has no statewide source-of-income protection law, and the City of Cleveland does not require landlords to accept vouchers; only Cleveland Heights, South Euclid, University Heights, Warrensville Heights, and Linndale have local ordinances that do. Apply for a voucher through CMHA, and see our full Cleveland Section 8 guide for the details.
