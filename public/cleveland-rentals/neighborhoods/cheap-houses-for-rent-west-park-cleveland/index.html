Renting a cheap house in West Park

West Park is not a single official neighborhood but a well-known umbrella term for three of Cleveland's far west-side Statistical Planning Areas: Kamm's Corners, Jefferson, and Bellaire-Puritas. Together they sit along the far western edge of the city, near the Cleveland Hopkins International Airport corridor and Interstate 71.

Kamm's Corners is the area's main commercial node, centered on Lorain Avenue and Rocky River Drive, with a mix of small shops and older residential streets branching off in every direction. Jefferson and Bellaire-Puritas are largely residential, with housing stock dating mostly from the early-to-mid 20th century — modest single-family homes, bungalows, and a smaller share of duplexes compared to Cleveland's east side.

How much does a cheap house cost in Cleveland?

"Cheap" is relative, so it helps to see the citywide range. Per Zumper's rent report dated July 4, 2026, Cleveland's overall median asking rent is $1,250 a month, up 4.2% year-over-year — and Zumper puts Cleveland roughly 36% below the national median of $1,950. A separate report from RentCafe (Yardi), dated July 2, 2026, tracks a higher average of $1,564 for professionally managed apartment buildings specifically, which tend to skew larger and newer than the individual houses and duplexes common on the far west side.

For comparison, the rental homes we manage across Greater Cleveland — mostly 2- and 3-bedroom houses and duplexes — typically run from about $700 to $1,800 a month, with a company-wide typical rent around $1,000. Every home in that portfolio accepts Housing Choice Vouchers (Section 8) and is HUD-inspection-ready.

Getting around West Park

West Park is served by Greater Cleveland RTA bus routes connecting Kamm's Corners and the surrounding streets to Downtown, and its location near Cleveland Hopkins International Airport puts it close to the RTA Red Line, the only U.S. rapid-transit line that runs directly from a downtown core to its city's main airport. Lorain Avenue functions as the area's primary east-west commercial corridor.

  • Area: Kamm's Corners, Jefferson, and Bellaire-Puritas Statistical Planning Areas
  • Transit: RTA bus routes to Downtown; near the Red Line and Cleveland Hopkins Airport
  • Commercial hub: Lorain Avenue at Rocky River Drive (Kamm's Corners)
  • Highway access: Near Interstate 71

Is Section 8 accepted for houses in West Park?

Acceptance of Housing Choice Vouchers in Cleveland depends on the individual landlord. Ohio has no statewide source-of-income protection law, and as of this writing, the City of Cleveland itself has not enacted an ordinance protecting voucher holders from being turned away for using Section 8 — a handful of Cuyahoga County suburbs (including Cleveland Heights, South Euclid, University Heights, and Warrensville Heights) have their own local protections, but the city proper does not. Renters should confirm a landlord's voucher policy directly before applying.

Every home we manage across the Cleveland area accepts Housing Choice Vouchers and is HUD-inspection-ready, regardless of neighborhood.
