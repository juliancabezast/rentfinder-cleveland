Renting a house in Detroit-Shoreway

Detroit-Shoreway is a near-west-side Cleveland neighborhood in ZIP code 44102, running roughly along the Detroit Avenue corridor between Cudell and Ohio City, about three miles west of Downtown. The neighborhood contains the Gordon Square Arts District and sits a short drive from the West Shoreway (Route 2) along Lake Erie.

Housing stock is a mix of early-1900s two-family duplexes and single-family houses on blocks around W. 58th, W. 65th, and W. 76th Streets, with front porches and small yards typical of Cleveland's near-west side. We manage Section 8-friendly rental homes in and around Detroit-Shoreway as part of our broader West Side inventory — book a showing to see what's currently available.

How much does it cost to rent a house in Detroit-Shoreway?

Citywide, a 2-bedroom home runs a median of about $1,100 a month and a 3-bedroom about $1,350, per Zumper's rent report dated July 2026 — a reasonable starting point for Detroit-Shoreway. Homes in our own portfolio in this part of Cleveland typically run from about $700 to $1,800 a month depending on size and condition, with most renting closer to $1,000.

RentCafe's report dated July 2026 on professionally managed Cleveland apartments shows a citywide average of $1,564 a month; that figure leans toward newer managed buildings rather than the individually owned houses and duplexes more typical of Detroit-Shoreway's residential streets.

Getting around Detroit-Shoreway

Detroit-Shoreway is served by GCRTA bus routes running along Detroit Avenue connecting to Downtown and Lakewood, and sits close to Red Line rail service a short drive or bus ride south near Ohio City. The West Shoreway (Route 2) gives quick highway access west toward Lakewood and Westlake or east into Downtown.

  • ZIP code: 44102
  • Transit: Detroit Avenue bus routes to Downtown; Red Line access nearby via Ohio City
  • Nearby: Gordon Square Arts District, Battery Park green space
  • Highway access: West Shoreway (Route 2) to Downtown and western suburbs

Section 8 and vouchers in Detroit-Shoreway

Detroit-Shoreway (44102) is one of the ZIP codes where we currently manage rental homes, and every one of them accepts Housing Choice Vouchers (Section 8) and is HUD-inspection-ready. Ohio has no statewide law requiring landlords to accept vouchers, and the City of Cleveland does not currently have its own source-of-income protection ordinance — but our policy welcomes vouchers on every home we manage, no exceptions.

If you have a CMHA voucher and want to see what's available in or near Detroit-Shoreway, book a free showing, or read our Cleveland Section 8 guide for details on applying, waitlists, and inspections. CMHA's own Housing Choice Voucher application page has current program details.
