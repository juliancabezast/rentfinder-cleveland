Renting a house in Edgewater

Edgewater is a West Side Cleveland neighborhood along the Lake Erie shoreline, bordered by Cudell and Detroit-Shoreway to the south and the city of Lakewood to the west. The neighborhood is best known for Edgewater Park, part of the Cleveland Metroparks Lakefront Reservation, which runs along the water's edge with a beach, bluff-top trails, and picnic areas open to the public.

Housing in Edgewater is a mix of early-1900s single-family homes and duplexes on residential streets set back from the park, generally similar in age and style to housing in neighboring Cudell and Detroit-Shoreway. Because it's a smaller lakefront pocket, standalone house listings directly in Edgewater are limited at any given time — tell our leasing team what you're looking for and we can point you to comparable West Side homes.

How much does it cost to rent a house in Edgewater?

There's no rent index published specifically for Edgewater, so the most useful reference is Cleveland's citywide rent data by bedroom count. Per Zumper's rent report dated July 2026, the citywide median is about $1,100 a month for a 2-bedroom home and about $1,350 for a 3-bedroom, up 4.2% year-over-year. Lakefront-adjacent West Side neighborhoods like Edgewater often price toward the middle or upper half of these citywide ranges.

RentCafe's report dated July 2026 on professionally managed Cleveland apartments shows a citywide average of $1,564 a month for a unit averaging 787 square feet — a helpful ceiling reference, since that figure reflects newer managed buildings rather than the older individual houses typical of Edgewater's residential streets.

Getting around Edgewater

Edgewater is served by GCRTA bus routes connecting to Downtown and along the West Side lakefront corridor, and the West Shoreway (Route 2) runs along the neighborhood's southern edge, giving quick highway access into Downtown or west toward Lakewood. Edgewater Park itself has a boat launch, fishing pier, and paved paths connecting to the regional Lakefront Reservation trail system.

  • Borders: Cudell and Detroit-Shoreway to the south; Lakewood to the west
  • Transit: GCRTA bus routes to Downtown; West Shoreway (Route 2) along the southern edge
  • Outdoors: Edgewater Park (Cleveland Metroparks Lakefront Reservation) — beach, bluff trails, boat launch
  • Nearby: Whiskey Island, a short drive east along the shoreline
