What is the Bellaire-Puritas neighborhood?

Bellaire-Puritas is a far-west-side Cleveland neighborhood positioned along the I-71 corridor near Cleveland Hopkins International Airport, generally covered by ZIP code 44135. It's named for two of its main streets, Bellaire Road and Puritas Avenue, which run through the district and connect it to the neighboring West Park and Riverside areas.

The neighborhood developed alongside the growth of the airport and the interstate system in the mid-1900s, and its housing stock reflects that era: single-family ranch and colonial-style homes on suburban-scale lots, with some duplexes mixed in. That later construction period sets Bellaire-Puritas apart from Cleveland neighborhoods closer to downtown, many of which were built decades earlier around streetcar lines rather than highway access.

Getting around Bellaire-Puritas

Bellaire-Puritas sits directly along the I-71/Cleveland Hopkins Airport corridor, making it one of the most highway-accessible far-west-side neighborhoods for drivers. GCRTA bus routes run along Bellaire Road and Puritas Avenue connecting to downtown and neighboring suburbs, per GCRTA's published route network, and the Red Line's West Park and Triskett rapid stations are within reach for riders heading toward downtown Cleveland's Tower City hub or the airport.

  • ZIP code: Generally 44135, on Cleveland's far west side
  • Highways: Directly along the I-71/Cleveland Hopkins Airport corridor
  • Transit: GCRTA bus routes on Bellaire Rd and Puritas Ave; nearby Red Line West Park and Triskett stations
  • Green space: Near the Rocky River Reservation (Cleveland Metroparks)

What kind of housing is available in Bellaire-Puritas?

Housing in Bellaire-Puritas mostly dates to the mid-1900s, with single-family ranch and colonial-style homes on larger, more suburban-style lots than found closer to downtown, along with a smaller share of duplexes. Attached garages and small side yards are more common here than in Cleveland's older streetcar-era neighborhoods, a byproduct of the area's later, more auto-oriented development pattern near the interstate.
