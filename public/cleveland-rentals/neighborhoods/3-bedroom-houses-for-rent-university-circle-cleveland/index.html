How much does a 3-bedroom house rent for near here?

Citywide, Zumper's rent report dated July 4, 2026 puts Cleveland's median 3-bedroom asking rent at $1,350 a month. RentCafe (Yardi), reporting July 2, 2026, shows a much higher $2,716 average for 3-bedroom units — but that figure reflects larger professionally managed apartment buildings near University Circle's institutional core, not the smaller houses found in the surrounding residential streets.

In nearby Hough and Glenville, where we do work with rental homes, currently available 3-bedroom houses across our Cleveland selection generally run $1,100 to $1,500 a month, tracking closer to the Zumper citywide median than the large-apartment RentCafe figure.

Transit around University Circle

University Circle is one of the best-connected parts of Cleveland for transit. The RTA Red Line (heavy rail) runs through the area on its route between Cleveland Hopkins Airport, Downtown's Tower City, and Windermere in East Cleveland. The HealthLine, a bus rapid transit route running in a dedicated center median along Euclid Avenue, connects Public Square directly to University Circle and on to East Cleveland — making it possible to reach the area's institutions without a car from many parts of the East Side.

  • Key institutions: Case Western Reserve University, Cleveland Museum of Art, Severance Music Center
  • Rail: RTA Red Line (heavy rail)
  • Bus rapid transit: HealthLine along Euclid Avenue
  • Adjacent neighborhoods with rentals we work with: Hough and Glenville

Voucher-friendly 3-bedroom homes near University Circle

Every rental home we work with in Hough and Glenville — the neighborhoods bordering University Circle — accepts Housing Choice Vouchers (Section 8) and is HUD-inspection-ready. Ohio has no statewide source-of-income protection law, and Cleveland has not adopted a citywide ordinance requiring landlords to accept vouchers, so it's worth confirming a specific landlord's policy if you're looking outside what we work with.
