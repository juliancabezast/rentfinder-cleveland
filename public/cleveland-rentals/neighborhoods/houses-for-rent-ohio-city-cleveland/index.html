How much does it cost to rent a house in Ohio City?

Cleveland-wide rent data gives a useful baseline for Ohio City house-hunters, though actual asking rents vary by home condition and size. Per Zumper's July 4, 2026 report, Cleveland's overall median asking rent is $1,250/month (up 4.2% year-over-year), with 2-bedroom units at $1,100 and 3-bedroom units at $1,350. RentCafe (Yardi), reporting July 2, 2026, puts the citywide average across professionally managed apartments at $1,564/month for 787 sq. ft. — higher because that figure leans toward larger managed complexes rather than smaller rental houses.

Rent Finder Cleveland's own portfolio of 90+ Section 8-ready homes across Greater Cleveland typically runs $700 to $1,800 a month, with most homes renting around $1,000. That range reflects mostly 2- and 3-bedroom homes, which make up the bulk of what we manage.

Getting around Ohio City

Ohio City sits along the near-west side of the Cuyahoga River, adjacent to the Detroit-Shoreway neighborhood and its Gordon Square Arts District. The RTA Red Line's Ohio City station connects the neighborhood directly to Downtown Cleveland (Tower City) on one side and Cleveland Hopkins International Airport on the other, with trains running roughly every 15 minutes throughout the day.

  • ZIP code: 44113
  • Transit: RTA Red Line — Ohio City station; connecting bus routes to Downtown and near-west-side neighborhoods
  • Landmark: West Side Market, one of Cleveland's oldest public markets
  • Nearby neighborhoods: Detroit-Shoreway/Gordon Square, Tremont, Downtown

Section 8 and housing vouchers in Ohio City

Housing Choice Voucher (Section 8) holders can apply for the program year-round through the Cuyahoga Metropolitan Housing Authority (CMHA), which administers vouchers for Cuyahoga County. Applicants apply online through CMHA's Housing Choice Voucher portal, and selection runs on a rolling mini-lottery basis rather than strict first-come, first-served.

One honesty note for renters: Ohio has no statewide source-of-income law, and as of this writing the City of Cleveland — which includes Ohio City — has not enacted a local ordinance requiring landlords to accept vouchers (a small number of Cuyahoga County suburbs, such as Cleveland Heights, do have such protections). That said, every home Rent Finder Cleveland manages, including those in and around Ohio City, accepts Section 8 vouchers and is HUD-inspection-ready by our own policy, regardless of what local law requires.
