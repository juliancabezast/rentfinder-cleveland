What is Kamm's Corners?

Kamm's Corners is the commercial hub at the intersection of Lorain Avenue and Rocky River Drive on Cleveland's far west side, within the larger West Park statistical planning area. The name refers to both the business district — a strip of shops and restaurants along Lorain Avenue — and the surrounding residential blocks.

Housing around Kamm's Corners consists mostly of early-1900s single-family homes on tree-lined side streets, a housing stock typical of Cleveland's west-side streetcar-suburb era. The district sits close to the city's western border with Fairview Park, and its commercial strip hosts an annual outdoor festival and a seasonal farmers market that draw visitors from nearby west-side neighborhoods.

What should renters know before signing a lease near Kamm's Corners?

Ohio law generally requires a landlord to return a tenant's security deposit within 30 days after the lease ends and the tenant moves out and provides a forwarding address, along with an itemized list of any deductions, under Ohio Revised Code 5321.16. If a deposit exceeds $50 or one month's rent (whichever is greater) and the tenant stayed six months or more, the landlord generally owes 5% annual interest on the excess. Renters who feel a deposit was wrongfully withheld may be entitled to recover double the amount plus attorney's fees under the same statute — this is general information, not legal advice, so verify current details with the Ohio Revised Code or a local tenant-rights resource before acting.
