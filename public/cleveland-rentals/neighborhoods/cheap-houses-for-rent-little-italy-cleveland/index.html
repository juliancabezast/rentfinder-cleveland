Cheap Houses for Rent in Little Italy

Little Italy's small footprint and closeness to University Circle keep house rents near the district close to Cleveland's citywide averages rather than at the cheap end. Renters on a tight budget typically find lower-cost houses a few miles east, in neighborhoods such as Hough or Glenville, both reachable by the same transit lines.

Is there a cheap house to rent in Little Italy?

Little Italy is a small district centered on Mayfield Road, wedged between Lake View Cemetery and University Circle about five miles east of Downtown Cleveland. Its housing stock is dense, older, and limited in supply, and its closeness to Case Western Reserve University, the Cleveland Museum of Art, and the Cleveland Clinic keeps demand — and prices — closer to Cleveland's citywide averages than to the cheap end of the market.

Renters specifically looking for a lower-cost house usually do better widening the search a few miles out. Tell our leasing team your budget and we'll be upfront about whether anything near Little Italy currently fits, or point you toward comparable, more affordable options nearby.

Cleveland rent benchmarks for budget-minded renters

Per Zumper's rent report dated July 4, 2026, Cleveland's overall median rent is $1,250 a month, up 4.2% year-over-year, and about 36% cheaper than the U.S. national median of $1,950. RentCafe's July 2, 2026 report on professionally managed apartments — more common near University Circle than in older neighborhoods — puts the citywide average considerably higher, at $1,564 for 787 square feet.

Rent Finder Cleveland's own rental homes across Greater Cleveland typically run from about $700 to $1,800 a month, with most landing around $1,000 — a range worth using as your baseline when comparing any specific listing near Little Italy.

Lower-cost neighborhoods within reach of Little Italy

A few miles from Little Italy, Cleveland's east side has neighborhoods with a larger supply of houses at lower price points, including Hough between Downtown and University Circle, and Glenville to the northeast near St. Clair Avenue. Both fall within Rent Finder Cleveland's core service area, alongside Fairfax near the Cleveland Clinic's main campus.

  • Hough: ZIP 44103, east side, between Downtown and University Circle
  • Glenville: ZIP 44108, northeast side, near St. Clair Avenue
  • Fairfax: ZIP 44104, east side, adjacent to the Cleveland Clinic main campus
  • Transit: HealthLine and RTA bus routes connect all three to University Circle and Downtown

Saving on transportation near Little Italy

Little Italy sits directly on two of Cleveland's strongest transit corridors: the RTA Red Line heavy rail, serving the University Circle station with a direct line to Downtown's Tower City and Cleveland Hopkins International Airport, and the HealthLine bus rapid transit along Euclid Avenue. Renting slightly further out and relying on either line can offset a chunk of what a car costs to own and park in a dense district like this one.

Section 8 vouchers as a way to lower monthly cost

Housing Choice Voucher (Section 8) applicants can apply year-round through the Cuyahoga Metropolitan Housing Authority's online portal; selection runs through periodic mini-lotteries rather than a simple waitlist line, and CMHA's published income limit for a single-person household, effective April 3, 2025, is $34,800.
