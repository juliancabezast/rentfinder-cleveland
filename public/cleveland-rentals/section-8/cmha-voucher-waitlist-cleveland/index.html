CMHA Housing Choice Voucher Waitlist — How It Works

The CMHA Housing Choice Voucher preliminary application list is open year-round in Cuyahoga County — there's no closing date to watch for. Instead of a simple first-come-first-served line, CMHA selects applicants through periodic random lotteries, so wait times vary widely, from a few days to several months.

Updated July 4, 2026 ·4 min read ·By the Rent Finder Cleveland team

Is the CMHA Section 8 waitlist open right now?

Yes. As of 2026-07-04, CMHA's Housing Choice Voucher preliminary application intake is open year-round with no closing date — you can submit an application at any time rather than waiting for a specific enrollment window to open. That said, an 'open' list is not the same as an immediately available voucher; it just means you can get into the pool whenever you're ready.

How does CMHA decide who gets a voucher next?

CMHA does not work through applications in strict date order. Instead, it draws from its pool of preliminary applications using periodic random 'mini-lotteries.' That means two people who applied on the same day can have very different outcomes, and being an early applicant does not guarantee an early voucher. CMHA describes the range as anywhere from a few days to several months, depending on the lottery draw. Treat any specific 'X people invited per month' figure you see elsewhere as unverified — CMHA has not published a fixed monthly invitation number, and reported figures vary by source.

How do I check my CMHA application status?

Contact CMHA's Applications Team directly at 216-431-1471, option 6, or through the applicant portal at cmha.net, to check where your preliminary application stands. CMHA's Main Campus (8120 Kinsman Road, Cleveland, OH 44104) also has computer kiosks if you need in-person help checking your status or completing paperwork.

If you haven't applied yet, see our step-by-step guide to applying for Section 8 through CMHA — since the list is open year-round, there's no reason to wait to submit your preliminary application.

What should I do while I'm waiting for a voucher?

Waiting periods are unpredictable, so it helps to prepare in parallel rather than pause your housing search entirely. Confirm your household's income falls under CMHA's current Very-Low-Income limits, keep your contact information (especially your email) up to date with CMHA so you don't miss a lottery notification, and start getting familiar with the rental market so you're ready to move quickly once a voucher is issued.

Why does the CMHA waitlist feel so unpredictable?

The lottery model exists because far more households apply than CMHA has vouchers to issue in any given cycle, so a strict first-come-first-served line would still leave most applicants waiting indefinitely with no visibility into their position. A periodic random draw at least gives every open preliminary application a fresh chance each cycle, rather than locking later applicants permanently behind everyone who applied before them. The tradeoff is that no one — not even CMHA — can tell you in advance exactly when your name will come up, which is why the honest answer to 'how long will it take' is a range, not a date.

It's worth separating CMHA's voucher waitlist from the process of finding a home once you have a voucher — those are two different waiting periods, and the second one is much more within your control. Spending the waitlist period preparing your paperwork and getting familiar with typical Cleveland rents means you won't lose time once your name is finally drawn.

Frequently asked questions

Is the CMHA waitlist currently open?
Yes. As of 2026-07-04, CMHA accepts preliminary Housing Choice Voucher applications year-round with no closing date. You can apply any time rather than waiting for an announced opening, though getting on the list does not mean a voucher is immediately available.
How long does the CMHA waitlist take?
There's no fixed timeline. CMHA selects applicants through periodic random lotteries rather than strict application order, so wait times reportedly range from a few days to several months. Applying earlier does not guarantee being selected sooner, so it's worth applying as soon as you're eligible.
Does CMHA invite a set number of people each month?
CMHA has not published a fixed monthly invitation number that we could verify. Some third-party sites mention approximate figures, but treat those as unconfirmed until you see them on CMHA's own materials or hear it directly from CMHA staff at 216-431-1471, option 6.
How do I check my status on the waitlist?
Call CMHA's Applications Team at 216-431-1471, option 6, or check the applicant portal at cmha.net. You can also visit CMHA's Main Campus at 8120 Kinsman Road, Cleveland, OH 44104, which has kiosks available for applicants who don't have reliable internet access at home.
Can I look for a home before I have a voucher?
Yes. You can tour and get familiar with available homes while your application is pending. Every home we work with accepts Section 8, so once your voucher is issued you can move directly to applying without wondering whether a listing will accept it.
