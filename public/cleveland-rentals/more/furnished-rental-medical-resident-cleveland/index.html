Furnished short-term rentals for medical residents in Cleveland

Medical residents starting at Cleveland Clinic or University Hospitals typically look for furnished, short-term rentals near the Fairfax and University Circle areas, close to both institutions, with a lease length matched to a rotation or the first year of residency rather than a standard 12-month term. Availability and furnishing vary property by property.

Updated July 4, 2026 ·4 min read ·By the Rent Finder Cleveland team

What do medical residents need in a rental near Cleveland's hospitals?

Cleveland's two largest employers are healthcare systems — Cleveland Clinic, the region's top employer, and University Hospitals (UH), the county's second-largest — per Crain's Cleveland Business's 2025 largest-employers data. Residents relocating for a program at either system generally want three things a standard year-long unfurnished lease doesn't always offer: furniture already in place, a lease length that can match a rotation schedule rather than locking into a full year, and reasonable proximity to campus for early call shifts.

Not every rental home is set up this way — furnished units and short-term terms are the exception, not the rule, in most Cleveland rental listings, so it's worth confirming both details directly with a landlord or property manager before assuming they're available.

Where are Cleveland Clinic and UH relative to the neighborhoods with rental homes?

Cleveland Clinic's main campus sits in the Fairfax neighborhood, east of Downtown; University Hospitals' main campus is in University Circle, home to Case Western Reserve University and several cultural institutions, and served by the Red Line rail plus the HealthLine bus-rapid-transit route along Euclid Avenue. Hough, between Downtown and University Circle, and Glenville, just north of University Circle, both sit within a short commute of both hospital systems.

We manage rental homes in several of these East Cleveland neighborhoods, including Fairfax (44104), Hough (44103), and Glenville (44108) — all within the general vicinity of both hospital campuses.

Furnished vs. unfurnished, short-term vs. standard lease

The tradeoffs are straightforward: a furnished, short-term rental costs more per month than an unfurnished 12-month lease at a comparable address, because the landlord is absorbing furniture costs and turnover risk over a shorter commitment. A standard unfurnished lease is cheaper monthly but requires the resident to buy or bring furniture and typically commit to a full year.

Timing your search around Match Day and orientation

Because residency start dates are fixed by Match Day and program orientation schedules, most incoming residents have a narrow window to secure housing before relocating — often while still finishing a prior program out of state. That timeline makes remote scheduling important: ask any Cleveland landlord or property manager whether they can arrange a video walkthrough or a single in-person visit scheduled around a trip to sign a lease, rather than requiring multiple return visits before move-in.

It's also worth confirming move-in timing against your specific rotation start date in writing, since a gap of even a few days between when a unit is ready and when your program begins can mean paying for temporary housing on top of your new lease.
