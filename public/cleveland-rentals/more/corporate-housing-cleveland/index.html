Corporate Housing in Cleveland, Ohio

What is corporate housing, and how does it work in Cleveland?

Corporate housing is a furnished rental — usually a house, duplex unit, or apartment — leased for a set project or relocation period, commonly 30 days to several months, with utilities, basic furniture, and sometimes housewares included in one monthly rate. It exists to bridge the gap between a hotel stay and a standard 12-month lease.

In Cleveland, corporate housing is most often booked by employees relocating for jobs at large regional employers, contractors on multi-month projects, or companies housing staff during training or transition periods. Cleveland Clinic and University Hospitals, the region's two largest employers, generate a steady stream of this kind of temporary-housing demand, as do downtown corporate offices and Case Western Reserve University-adjacent research institutions.

Where our rental homes are located

Our portfolio is concentrated in East and Southeast Cleveland, including Slavic Village (ZIP 44105), Collinwood (44110), Glenville (44108), Fairfax/Central (44104), Hough (44103), and the Buckeye-Shaker Square area (44120), with a smaller number of homes on the West side around Cudell/Detroit-Shoreway (44102) and Old Brooklyn (44109). Fairfax (44104) sits directly adjacent to the Cleveland Clinic main campus, and Hough (44103) sits between Downtown and University Circle, which is useful context if your temporary-housing need is tied to a hospital or downtown assignment.

We also manage a smaller number of homes in Akron, Lorain, and Elyria, Ohio, outside the core Cleveland footprint.

What does corporate housing cost compared to a standard lease?

Furnished, all-inclusive corporate housing generally carries a premium over an unfurnished long-term lease of a comparable size, because the monthly rate bundles furniture, utilities, and shorter minimum stays into one price.
