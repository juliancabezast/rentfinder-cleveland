Residential vs. commercial leasing: what's the difference?

Commercial leasing generally covers space used for business operations — storefronts, restaurants, professional offices, warehouses, and light-industrial space — governed by commercial lease terms (often triple-net or modified gross leases, longer terms, and build-out negotiations). Residential leasing, what we do, covers houses and apartments used as a tenant's primary home, governed in Ohio primarily by landlord-tenant statute (Ohio Revised Code Chapter 5321), not commercial lease law.

Where to find commercial space in Cleveland

Commercial space typically moves through commercial real estate brokerages, landlords who own dedicated commercial buildings, and local economic-development contacts rather than a residential rental team like ours. If you're scouting a storefront or office, a commercial broker who specializes in the neighborhood or corridor you have in mind will have access to listings, pricing comps, and lease terms that a residential manager simply doesn't track.

Cleveland has several established commercial and mixed-use corridors — for example, the Warehouse District and Gateway area downtown, and neighborhood commercial strips like Broadway and Fleet Avenues in Slavic Village or Lorain Avenue through Ohio City and West Park — but availability, square footage, and asking rates for those spaces are outside the data we track for residential rentals, so we won't guess at numbers here.

If you already have a specific commercial address in mind, the fastest path is usually contacting the listing broker directly, since commercial availability changes frequently and isn't centralized the way residential listings are on most rental search sites.

If you also need a place to live in Cleveland

It's common for someone opening or relocating a business to also need housing nearby. On the residential side, we work with 2- and 3-bedroom rental homes concentrated on Cleveland's East and Southeast side — including Slavic Village, Collinwood, Glenville, Fairfax/Central, Hough, and Buckeye-Shaker — plus a smaller number of West-side homes, with rents generally running from about $700 to $1,800 a month. Every home we work with accepts Section 8 / Housing Choice Vouchers and is HUD-inspection-ready.
