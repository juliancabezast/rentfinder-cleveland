Do Solon apartments accept Section 8 vouchers?

Housing Choice Vouchers (Section 8) throughout Cuyahoga County, including Solon, are administered by the Cuyahoga Metropolitan Housing Authority (CMHA). Whether a specific Solon building accepts a voucher is still up to that building's owner, because Ohio has no statewide source-of-income protection law. As of this writing, only Cleveland Heights, South Euclid, University Heights, Warrensville Heights, and Linndale have local ordinances protecting voucher holders from refusal — Solon is not one of them.

If you hold a voucher and are considering Solon, confirm acceptance with the landlord directly before applying. You can start or check a preliminary application through CMHA's Housing Choice Voucher program. Every home Rent Finder Cleveland works with accepts Section 8 and is HUD-inspection-ready — see our Cleveland Section 8 guide.

CMHA's preliminary voucher application has no closing date and stays open year-round, but placement runs through random mini-lottery drawings rather than a strict line, so timing varies from days to months. Applicants generally need an active email address, a Social Security number, and income details for each household member. Once a voucher is issued, the chosen unit — wherever it is in Cuyahoga County — must pass a physical inspection under HUD's HQS-to-NSPIRE standards before assistance begins.

Getting around Solon

Solon sits in the southeastern part of Cuyahoga County, near Bedford, Bedford Heights, and Warrensville Heights. GCRTA's rail network (Red, Blue, and Green lines) does not extend as far southeast as Solon, so residents typically rely on driving for both local trips and commutes toward downtown Cleveland or the University Circle area, where major regional employers like Cleveland Clinic and University Hospitals are based. Renters without a car should plan for a longer commute than in neighborhoods closer to the RTA rail network, since Solon has no direct rail or BRT connection of its own.

Lead paint disclosure for older rentals near Solon

If you're touring an older apartment near Solon, federal law is the relevant rule, not a city ordinance. Any residential rental built before 1978 — nationwide, including Solon — is covered by the federal Residential Lead-Based Paint Hazard Reduction Act, meaning the landlord generally must disclose any known lead-based paint or hazards, provide the EPA's lead-hazard pamphlet, and include lead-warning language in the lease.

Cleveland's own Lead-Safe Certification ordinance — which requires a separate inspection and certificate for pre-1978 rentals — is a City of Cleveland requirement and does not extend to Solon or other Cuyahoga County suburbs, which have their own local rules, if any. This is general information, not legal advice; verify current requirements before signing a lease on an older Solon apartment.
