Where Bedford, Ohio fits in the Cleveland area

Bedford is a Cuyahoga County suburb directly southeast of Cleveland, bordering Bedford Heights and reached from the city via the I-271 corridor. Part of the Cleveland Metroparks' Bedford Reservation, which follows Tinker's Creek, lies within the city, giving the area a mix of residential streets and Metroparks green space.

Housing stock in and around Bedford includes early-to-mid 20th-century single-family homes as well as smaller multi-family duplexes, similar to much of inner-ring Cuyahoga County.

Section 8 and Housing Choice Vouchers if you're searching near Bedford

The Cuyahoga Metropolitan Housing Authority (CMHA) administers Housing Choice Vouchers for all of Cuyahoga County, including Bedford. CMHA accepts preliminary applications year-round with no closing date, through its Housing Choice Voucher application page; selection then runs by periodic random lottery from that list rather than strict first-come, first-served, so a spot can come up within days or take months. Current CMHA income limits (effective April 3, 2025) are $34,800 for one person, $39,800 for two, $49,700 for four, and $65,650 for eight — always confirm current limits directly with CMHA.

Ohio has no statewide law requiring landlords to accept a voucher as a source of income, and Bedford is not among the small set of Cuyahoga County communities reported to have local voucher-protection ordinances (Cleveland Heights, South Euclid, University Heights, Warrensville Heights, and Linndale). Confirm a specific landlord's voucher policy directly before applying. Ohio renter protections that apply wherever you rent

Ohio's landlord-tenant statute applies statewide, including Bedford. Under ORC 5321.16, a security deposit exceeding $50 or one month's rent (whichever is greater) earns 5% annual interest if a tenant stays six months or more, and a landlord must return the deposit with an itemized statement of any deductions within 30 days of move-out. Before filing an eviction for nonpayment, Ohio law requires a landlord to serve a statutory 3-day notice under ORC 1923.04. This is general information, not legal advice — verify current statute language before relying on it.
