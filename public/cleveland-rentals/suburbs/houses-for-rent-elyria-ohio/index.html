Where Elyria, Ohio fits in the region

Elyria is the Lorain County seat, roughly 25 miles southwest of Cleveland via Route 2 and I-90. The city is built around the forks of the Black River, and Cascade Park runs along the river through downtown Elyria, including the East and West Falls of the Black River. Lorain County Community College sits just outside the city, in Elyria Township.

Elyria's housing stock includes early-20th-century homes near downtown along with mid-century subdivisions further out, a mix common to Lorain County's county-seat cities. Elyria has its own Amtrak station served by the Lake Shore Limited, and Route 254 connects the city toward the Cleveland Hopkins International Airport area to the northeast.

Section 8 vouchers for a house in Elyria

Elyria is in Lorain County, not Cuyahoga County, so the Cuyahoga Metropolitan Housing Authority (CMHA) — which administers vouchers for Cleveland and Cuyahoga County — is not the agency covering Elyria addresses. HUD's Housing Choice Voucher program overview explains how the nationwide program works and can help you locate the public housing authority responsible for a Lorain County address.

Whichever agency issues your voucher, every home Rent Finder Cleveland works with — including our Elyria-area homes — accepts Section 8 and is HUD-inspection-ready. Read our full Cleveland Section 8 guide for how vouchers, inspections, and applications work with our available homes.

Ohio landlord-tenant law that applies in Elyria

Ohio's landlord-tenant statute applies statewide, including Elyria. Under ORC 5321.16, a security deposit exceeding $50 or one month's rent (whichever is greater) earns 5% annual interest once a tenant has stayed six months or more, and must be returned with an itemized statement within 30 days of move-out. Before filing an eviction for nonpayment, a landlord must first serve a statutory 3-day notice under ORC 1923.04. This is general information, not legal advice — verify current statute language before relying on it.
