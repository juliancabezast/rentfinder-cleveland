Renting a house in Berea

Berea is a Cuyahoga County suburb southwest of downtown Cleveland, most widely known as the home of Baldwin Wallace University. The area mixes owner-occupied single-family blocks near the campus with rental houses and duplexes scattered throughout its residential streets.

Berea's downtown historic district, centered around Front Street and Bagley Road, sits within walking distance of the university and includes small storefronts, restaurants, and Coe Lake Park. Renters who work in Berea but want a shorter commute to downtown Cleveland or the airport sometimes widen their search to neighboring suburbs and Cleveland's west side, where rental houses tend to be more numerous.

How much does it cost to rent a house near Berea?

Berea doesn't have its own published rent index, so renters generally use the wider Cleveland-area market as a reference point. Zumper's July 4, 2026 report placed the area's median asking rent at $1,250 a month, up 4.2% year-over-year, while RentCafe's July 2, 2026 report — which weights toward larger professionally-managed communities — found a higher average of $1,564 for 787 square feet. Expect an individual Berea house's rent to depend heavily on its size, age, and proximity to Baldwin Wallace's campus.

Getting around Berea

Berea sits along the I-71 corridor southwest of Cleveland, giving quick highway access toward downtown and toward Cleveland Hopkins International Airport a few miles to the northeast. GCRTA, the region's public transit agency, operates bus routes connecting Cuyahoga County suburbs like Berea to the wider Cleveland transit network, though Berea isn't served directly by RTA's rail lines.

  • Highway: I-71 corridor, southwest of downtown Cleveland
  • Airport: A few miles from Cleveland Hopkins International Airport
  • Transit: GCRTA bus routes; no direct rail line into Berea
  • Landmark: Baldwin Wallace University

Section 8 and housing vouchers in Berea

As in the rest of Ohio, there's no state law requiring a landlord to accept Housing Choice Vouchers (Section 8), and Berea is not currently one of the Cuyahoga County suburbs — Cleveland Heights, South Euclid, University Heights, Warrensville Heights, and Linndale — that have passed their own source-of-income protection ordinance as of July 2026. Ask directly about a listing's voucher policy before applying. CMHA runs the county's Housing Choice Voucher program, including for renters looking in Berea, and its preliminary application stays open year-round with placement by rolling lottery. Every home we manage accepts Section 8 and is HUD-inspection-ready — read more in our Section 8 housing guide.

Utilities in Berea

Electric service in Berea generally comes from The Illuminating Company (FirstEnergy) rather than Cleveland Public Power, which is limited mostly to addresses inside Cleveland's city limits. Natural gas is typically provided by Enbridge Gas Ohio (formerly Dominion East Ohio), though some Cuyahoga County suburbs use Columbia Gas of Ohio instead — confirm which one serves your address. Cleveland Water, one of the country's largest municipal water systems, supplies many suburbs in addition to the city itself.

Climate and moving considerations near Berea

Like most of Cleveland's west side, Berea sits outside the region's heaviest lake-effect snow belt, which concentrates in the higher-elevation areas south and east of Lake Erie. The Cleveland area overall averages roughly 60–70 inches of snow a season, with lake-effect activity heaviest from November through January, but west-side and southwest suburbs like Berea typically see less accumulation than communities on the city's higher-elevation East Side. Renters moving in winter should still plan for regular snow removal and confirm in the lease whether snow and lawn service are included.
