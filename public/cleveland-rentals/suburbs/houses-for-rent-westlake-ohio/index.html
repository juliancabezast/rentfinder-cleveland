What does it cost to rent a house near Westlake?

Westlake isn't tracked as its own line item in the rent reports we use, and suburban houses here often run above citywide Cleveland figures given the area's newer housing stock and larger lot sizes. Two different market reports illustrate why it helps to check more than one source: Zumper reports median asking rents, while RentCafe reports averages weighted toward larger, professionally-managed buildings.

Zumper's report, dated July 4, 2026, put the Cleveland-area median at $1,250/mo, with 2-bedroom homes averaging around $1,100 and 3-bedroom homes around $1,350. RentCafe's (Yardi) report, dated July 2, 2026, put the broader average noticeably higher, at $1,564/mo across roughly 787 square feet. Neither figure is Westlake-specific, but together they bracket a reasonable range to expect before you start touring.

Getting around Westlake and commuting for work

Westlake is within Cuyahoga County and therefore inside the GCRTA service area, which operates suburban bus routes alongside its Red, Blue, Green, and Waterfront rail lines that all meet at Tower City downtown. Renters commuting from Westlake to downtown Cleveland or University Circle should check current GCRTA route coverage directly, since suburban bus service varies by street.

Westlake's location also matters for commute planning: it sits closer to west-side job centers and to Cleveland Hopkins-area traffic patterns than to University Circle, where the region's two largest employers, Cleveland Clinic and University Hospitals, are based. Renters weighing a Westlake address against an east-side commute should factor in the extra cross-town drive time, especially during peak highway congestion on I-90 or Route 2.

Utilities in the Westlake area

Electric service in Westlake generally comes from The Illuminating Company, since Cleveland Public Power is a municipal utility specific to the City of Cleveland itself. Natural gas in most of the Cleveland area now runs through Enbridge Gas Ohio, which completed its purchase of the former Dominion East Ohio system in March 2024, though a handful of suburbs still use Columbia Gas of Ohio — worth confirming for a specific address. Cleveland Water supplies many surrounding suburbs in addition to the city itself, and sewer/stormwater treatment across the metro generally runs through the Northeast Ohio Regional Sewer District. Confirm the exact providers for your address before budgeting move-in costs.

Section 8 vouchers in Westlake

Westlake is in Cuyahoga County, so Housing Choice Vouchers there are processed through the Cuyahoga Metropolitan Housing Authority (CMHA), the same agency that serves Cleveland. Applications run through CMHA's Housing Choice Voucher page, which accepts preliminary applications year-round and selects applicants through periodic random drawings rather than a fixed queue.

As with most of Cuyahoga County's western suburbs, Westlake has not passed a local ordinance protecting voucher holders from source-of-income discrimination; Ohio has no statewide protection either. That means a Westlake landlord can lawfully decline a Section 8 applicant, unlike in the handful of suburbs (Cleveland Heights, South Euclid, University Heights, Warrensville Heights, and Linndale, per Signal Cleveland reporting) that have local protections. Every home Rent Finder Cleveland works with accepts vouchers and is HUD-inspection-ready regardless of location; our Cleveland Section 8 guide covers the program in more detail.

Renting outside Cleveland's city limits

Westlake is a separate municipality from the City of Cleveland, so ordinances written specifically for Cleveland don't automatically extend here. Cleveland's Lead-Safe Certification law is a good example: it only applies to rental units within Cleveland's city limits, not to Westlake homes. Federal law still requires disclosure of known lead-based paint hazards in any home built before 1978, no matter which city or suburb it's in.

Ohio's statewide landlord-tenant law (Ohio Revised Code Chapter 5321) applies the same way in Westlake as it does in Cleveland proper — including the general rule that security deposits must be returned, with an itemized statement of any deductions, within 30 days of move-out. This is informational only, not legal advice; always verify the current statute before signing a lease.
