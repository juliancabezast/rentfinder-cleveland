What do rentals cost in the Greater Cleveland area right now?

We don't have Willowick-specific rent data, but regional figures give renters a useful benchmark. Per Zumper's rent research report dated July 4, 2026, the median asking rent across the Cleveland market is $1,250 a month, up 4.2% year-over-year. Per RentCafe's (Yardi) report dated July 2, 2026, the average rent across professionally managed Cleveland-area apartments is $1,564 a month for about 787 square feet — a higher figure because it weights larger, newer buildings rather than the full range of listings.

Section 8 and housing vouchers for Lake County renters

The Cuyahoga Metropolitan Housing Authority (CMHA) administers Housing Choice Vouchers (Section 8) for Cuyahoga County, which does not include Willowick — Willowick is in Lake County. If you hold a voucher and want to rent in Willowick, confirm with your caseworker which public housing authority has jurisdiction over Lake County addresses and whether porting your voucher there is an option.

It's also worth knowing that Ohio has no statewide source-of-income law requiring landlords to accept vouchers. As of our July 2026 research, only a handful of Cuyahoga County suburbs — Cleveland Heights, South Euclid, University Heights, Warrensville Heights, and Linndale — have local ordinances protecting voucher holders from that kind of denial. Nothing in our sources indicates a similar ordinance in Willowick or elsewhere in Lake County, so confirm current local rules before counting on a specific address.

Every home Rent Finder Cleveland manages accepts Housing Choice Vouchers and is HUD-inspection-ready — that's true across our entire portfolio, it's just that our portfolio sits in Cleveland proper rather than Lake County. Read more in our Cleveland Section 8 guide.
