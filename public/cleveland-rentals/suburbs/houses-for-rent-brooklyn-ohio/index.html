Renting a house in Brooklyn, Ohio

The City of Brooklyn is a Cuyahoga County suburb directly southwest of Cleveland, bordering the city rather than sitting inside it. It's easy to confuse with Cleveland's own Old Brooklyn and Brooklyn Centre neighborhoods, which are separate city SPAs (Statistical Planning Areas) a short distance away — Brooklyn the suburb is its own municipality with its own local government, distinct from those Cleveland neighborhood names.

What does it cost to rent a house near Brooklyn, Ohio?

Rents in the immediate Brooklyn/southwest Cleveland corridor vary by house size and condition, but as a Greater Cleveland-area benchmark, Zumper's July 4, 2026 report shows a metro median of about $1,100 for a 2-bedroom and $1,350 for a 3-bedroom home, while RentCafe's July 2, 2026 report — which weights toward larger managed buildings — shows higher averages across the board. Getting around Brooklyn and southwest Cleveland

Brooklyn sits just southwest of Cleveland, near the city's Old Brooklyn and Brooklyn Centre neighborhoods, which are served by Greater Cleveland RTA bus routes into downtown and sit near I-71 and I-480 for drivers. Old Brooklyn is also near the Cleveland Metroparks Zoo, one of the area's well-known public landmarks, and the broader area is within a short drive of Steelyard Commons and the West 25th Street corridor.

  • Location: Cuyahoga County suburb directly southwest of Cleveland
  • Not the same as: Cleveland's Old Brooklyn and Brooklyn Centre neighborhoods (ZIP 44109 area)
  • Transit nearby: RTA bus routes serving southwest Cleveland; I-71 and I-480 access
  • Nearby landmark: Cleveland Metroparks Zoo, adjacent to Old Brooklyn

Section 8 vouchers near Brooklyn

Ohio has no statewide law requiring landlords to accept Housing Choice Vouchers, so acceptance in the suburb of Brooklyn depends on the individual owner rather than a blanket local rule. Every home Rent Finder Cleveland works with accepts Section 8 / Housing Choice Vouchers and is HUD-inspection-ready, including our homes in Cleveland's nearby 44109 area (Old Brooklyn/Brooklyn Centre). The Cuyahoga Metropolitan Housing Authority (CMHA) administers the Housing Choice Voucher program countywide, including both Cleveland and its suburbs, and accepts preliminary applications year-round.
