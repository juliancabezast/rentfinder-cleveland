Renting a house in Euclid, Ohio

Euclid sits on Lake Erie directly northeast of Cleveland's Collinwood-Nottingham area, spanning ZIP codes that include 44117 and 44123. Much of the housing stock is mid-20th-century single-family and two-family homes, built up alongside the neighborhood's lakefront parks and its industrial corridor along the rail lines and I-90.

We manage 90+ voucher-friendly rental homes with our strongest concentration in Collinwood (ZIP 44110), which borders Euclid directly to the south — but Euclid itself is not currently part of our managed portfolio. If a home just across the border in Collinwood would work for you, tell us your bedroom count and budget and we'll show you what's currently available there.

Because Euclid sits at the far northeast edge of Cuyahoga County, it's also worth knowing the region is a snow belt: Cleveland's east side and the areas closer to the Lake/Cuyahoga County line typically see more lake-effect snowfall than west-side communities, with the heaviest totals generally between November and January while Lake Erie is still unfrozen. That's a practical factor to weigh alongside rent when comparing an east-side suburb like Euclid to a west-side one.

What does it cost to rent in the Cleveland area?

Euclid's own rents vary by block and home type, but as a Greater Cleveland benchmark, Zumper's July 4, 2026 report shows a metro median asking rent of $1,250/month, with 2-bedroom units around $1,100 and 3-bedroom units around $1,350. RentCafe's July 2, 2026 report, weighted toward larger professionally-managed apartment buildings, shows a higher metro average of $1,564/month for roughly 787 square feet.

Getting around Euclid

Euclid runs along the Lake Erie shoreline directly northeast of Cleveland's Collinwood-Nottingham neighborhood, with I-90 running through the city and providing a direct route into downtown Cleveland to the southwest. Greater Cleveland RTA bus routes connect Euclid to downtown along Euclid Avenue and Lakeshore Boulevard, and the city maintains lakefront parkland along its northern edge. For renters commuting to the region's largest employers, Cleveland Clinic and University Hospitals — the two biggest employers in the county — sit closer to the University Circle area southwest of Euclid, reachable via the same Euclid Avenue corridor the RTA HealthLine runs on further into the city.

Section 8 and voucher holders looking in Euclid

Ohio has no statewide law requiring landlords to accept Housing Choice Vouchers, so acceptance in Euclid depends on the individual property owner rather than a citywide mandate. What we can confirm about our own homes: every property Rent Finder Cleveland manages accepts Section 8 vouchers and is HUD-inspection-ready, and our closest concentration is right next door in Collinwood (ZIP 44110). The Cuyahoga Metropolitan Housing Authority (CMHA) administers the countywide Housing Choice Voucher program and accepts preliminary applications year-round; our Section 8 guide covers how the process generally works.
