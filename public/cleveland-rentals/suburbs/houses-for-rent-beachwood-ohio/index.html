Renting a house in Beachwood, Ohio

Beachwood is an east Cuyahoga County suburb bordering Shaker Heights to the west and Pepper Pike and Woodmere to the east, with ZIP code 44122 covering the city. Interstate 271 runs through Beachwood with an interchange at Chagrin Boulevard (U.S. Route 422), and Cedar Road and Richmond Road are additional major corridors. Housing stock ranges from mid-1900s single-family colonials in the city's residential neighborhoods to newer condominium and apartment developments along the Cedar Road and Chagrin Boulevard corridor.

What does it cost to rent in the Cleveland area?

Beachwood rents tend to run above the Cleveland city average given its housing mix and proximity to the Chagrin Boulevard commercial corridor, but as a general Greater Cleveland benchmark, Zumper's July 4, 2026 report puts the metro's median asking rent at $1,250/month overall, with 2-bedroom units around $1,100 and 3-bedroom units around $1,350. RentCafe's July 2, 2026 report, which weights toward larger professionally-managed buildings, shows a higher metro average of $1,564/month — a figure closer to what Beachwood's condo and apartment stock often reflects. Actual Beachwood house rents will depend on the specific home, its size, and condition.

Getting around Beachwood

Beachwood sits along Interstate 271 at the Chagrin Boulevard interchange, with Beachwood Place — a shopping center along Cedar Road near I-271 — serving as one of the city's main commercial anchors. Cedar Road and Richmond Road provide additional north-south and east-west routes through the city. Greater Cleveland RTA's fixed bus routes are concentrated closer to the city and inner-ring suburbs like Shaker Heights, so most Beachwood residents rely on a car for daily transportation.

Section 8 and voucher holders looking in Beachwood

Ohio has no statewide law requiring landlords to accept Housing Choice Vouchers, and Beachwood is not among the handful of Cuyahoga County communities — Cleveland Heights, South Euclid, University Heights, Warrensville Heights, and Linndale — that have adopted local source-of-income protection ordinances, so acceptance in Beachwood depends on the individual landlord. The Cuyahoga Metropolitan Housing Authority (CMHA) administers the countywide Housing Choice Voucher program and accepts preliminary applications year-round.
