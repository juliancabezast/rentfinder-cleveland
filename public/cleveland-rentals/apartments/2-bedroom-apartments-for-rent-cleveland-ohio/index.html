How much is a 2-bedroom apartment in Cleveland?

A two-bedroom apartment in Cleveland runs from about $1,100 to $1,818 a month, depending on the source. Zumper's July 4, 2026 report shows a $1,100 median asking rent for two-bedrooms citywide, while RentCafe's July 2, 2026 report — which tracks larger, professionally managed communities — shows a $1,818 average for roughly 1,027 square feet. Zumper's two-bedroom figure sitting at or below its own one-bedroom median ($1,195) reflects the current mix of listings in that report rather than an error; RentCafe's larger, newer-building sample runs meaningfully higher across every unit size.

Across both sources, Cleveland remains well below the national picture — Zumper's July 2026 report puts the U.S. median rent at $1,950 a month, about 36% above Cleveland's overall median.

Cleveland's 2-bedroom rental stock — houses, duplexes, and apartment buildings

Two-bedroom is the single most common home size in Cleveland's rental market, and it's also the backbone of our own portfolio: of the 90+ rental homes we manage across Greater Cleveland, more than half are 2-bedroom units. Rather than one large apartment complex, that inventory is spread across single-family houses, duplexes, triplexes, and fourplexes — so a 2-bedroom rental from us might be a full house, one half of a duplex, or a unit inside a small multi-family building, depending on the address.

By comparison, only about a quarter of our portfolio is 3-bedroom, and one- and larger-than-3-bedroom sizes make up the rest — which is why a renter specifically looking for two bedrooms tends to have the widest set of current options with us relative to any other home size.

Getting around from a 2-bedroom rental home

Several of these ZIP codes sit along useful transit corridors. The Buckeye-Shaker area (44120) runs along GCRTA's Blue and Green light-rail lines into Shaker Heights, while Hough (44103) and Central/Fairfax (44104) are within a few miles of University Circle and the Cleveland Clinic's main campus, reachable by the HealthLine bus rapid transit route on Euclid Avenue. Cleveland Clinic is the region's largest employer and University Hospitals is its second-largest, per Crain's Cleveland Business's 2025 employer rankings, which makes proximity to those campuses a common commute consideration for renters on the east side.

Collinwood (44110), on the city's northeast lakefront side, and Glenville (44108), closer to University Circle, are both reachable by GCRTA bus routes running into Downtown's Tower City rail hub, where the Red, Blue, Green, and Waterfront lines all connect. On the West side, Cudell/Detroit-Shoreway (44102) sits near the Gordon Square Arts District and is a shorter drive to Cleveland Hopkins International Airport via the Red Line or I-90.

Section 8 vouchers on 2-bedroom homes

Every one of our 90+ rental homes — 2-bedroom units included — accepts Section 8 Housing Choice Vouchers and is HUD-inspection-ready. That said, Ohio has no statewide source-of-income protection law, and the City of Cleveland does not currently require any landlord to accept a voucher; only a handful of Cuyahoga County suburbs, including Cleveland Heights, South Euclid, University Heights, and Warrensville Heights, have local ordinances that do. Check CMHA's Housing Choice Voucher page for current payment standards, or apply online once you've found a 2-bedroom home that fits. For the full picture, see our Cleveland Section 8 guide.

What a 2-bedroom rental home in Cleveland looks like

Because our 2-bedroom inventory spans single-family houses, duplexes, triplexes, and fourplexes rather than one uniform apartment style, features vary by address — some come with a basement and a fenced yard, typical of Cleveland's early-1900s housing stock, while a duplex unit might share a driveway or porch with a neighboring unit. Heating is a mix of forced-air furnaces and older radiator systems depending on the building's age. As with any Cleveland rental, it's worth confirming which utility providers serve a specific address — Cleveland Public Power or the Illuminating Company for electric, and Enbridge Gas Ohio for natural gas in most of the city — along with whether any utilities are included in rent before signing a lease.
