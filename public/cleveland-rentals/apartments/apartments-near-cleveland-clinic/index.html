Where is Cleveland Clinic's main campus?

Cleveland Clinic's main campus is in Fairfax, an east-side Cleveland neighborhood in ZIP code 44104, immediately adjacent to University Circle. Fairfax sits along the Euclid Avenue corridor served by the HealthLine, GCRTA's bus rapid transit route connecting Public Square downtown, the Midtown medical corridor, University Circle, and East Cleveland. Cleveland Clinic and University Hospitals together are the region's two largest employers, so demand for nearby housing comes heavily from medical staff, residents, and support workers on varied shift schedules.

Housing directly around the main campus ranges from larger apartment buildings to older single-family homes and duplexes converted to rentals. Fairfax borders Central, an east-side neighborhood just southeast of downtown with its own long housing history, and Hough to the north, both within a short distance of the Clinic's campus. Renters who want more room than a typical studio, or who are relocating with a household, often look slightly beyond the immediate campus blocks into these surrounding east-side neighborhoods, where the rental stock leans toward older single-family homes, duplexes, and small multi-family buildings rather than large new construction.

Getting to the Clinic without a car

The HealthLine's dedicated center-median busway makes it one of the more reliable transit options for shift workers commuting to Cleveland Clinic or University Hospitals at off-peak hours, since it runs in its own lane separate from general traffic along much of Euclid Avenue. The RTA Red Line, Cleveland's heavy-rail line, also serves the east side and connects to Tower City downtown, where riders can transfer to the Blue and Green light-rail lines toward Shaker Heights. Beyond rail and the HealthLine, GCRTA also runs a broader network of local bus routes across the east side, and paratransit service is available separately. Anyone commuting on a rotating shift schedule should confirm current GCRTA service hours directly with the agency, since frequency can drop late at night on some routes.
