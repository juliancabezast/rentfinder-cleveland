2-bedroom apartments in Ohio City

Ohio City is a near-west side Cleveland neighborhood in ZIP code 44113, directly across the Cuyahoga River from downtown, home to the historic West Side Market and served by GCRTA's Red Line at the Ohio City station. The neighborhood's housing stock is dominated by late-1800s Victorian-era houses, rowhouses, and duplexes, and a 2-bedroom layout is one of the most common results when these older buildings were carved into separate rental units — often with the higher ceilings, front porches, and full-size room proportions typical of that era, rather than a purpose-built modern floor plan.

A 2-bedroom in Ohio City works for roommates splitting rent, a household that wants a home office or guest room, or anyone who wants more space than a studio or 1-bedroom without leaving the neighborhood's walk-to-the-market convenience. How much does a 2-bedroom apartment cost in Ohio City?

Citywide, Zumper's July 4, 2026 report shows a median 2-bedroom rent of $1,100/month — notably close to the 1-bedroom median, which reflects the mix of smaller, older 2-bedroom conversions currently listed rather than a pricing error. RentCafe (Yardi)'s July 2, 2026 report, which weights toward larger professionally managed apartment communities averaging roughly 1,027 square feet for a 2-bedroom, shows a considerably higher citywide average of $1,818/month. In practice, an Ohio City 2-bedroom in an older rowhouse conversion often prices closer to the Zumper figure, while a unit in a newer managed building near the market district can run well above it.

Getting around from a 2-bedroom in Ohio City

Ohio City's own rapid-transit stop, the Ohio City station on RTA's Red Line, connects to downtown Tower City in one direction and toward Cleveland Hopkins Airport and the east side, including University Circle, in the other — a useful option for a household splitting a 2-bedroom who doesn't want two cars and two parking spots. West 25th Street is the neighborhood's main commercial spine, running past the West Side Market and Market Square Park, with I-90 access nearby for anyone driving.

Section 8 vouchers for a 2-bedroom in Ohio City

The Cuyahoga Metropolitan Housing Authority (CMHA) administers the Housing Choice Voucher (Section 8) program countywide, including Ohio City, through a year-round preliminary application and mini-lottery selection. Ohio has no statewide source-of-income law, and Cleveland has not enacted its own ordinance requiring landlords to accept vouchers, so acceptance for any specific 2-bedroom unit still depends on the individual landlord. Every rental home we manage in and around Ohio City accepts Section 8 vouchers and is HUD-inspection-ready — see our Cleveland Section 8 guide for how the CMHA process works.
