Renting an apartment near Cleveland State University

Cleveland State University sits within Cleveland's Downtown statistical planning area, the city's central business district anchored by Public Square and the Terminal Tower/Tower City rail hub, with the Gateway sports district and Warehouse District nearby. What does it cost to rent near Cleveland State?

There's no campus-specific rent index, so citywide figures are the closest available benchmark. Per Zumper's rent report dated July 4, 2026, Cleveland's citywide 1-bedroom median is $1,195/month and 2-bedroom is $1,100. Per RentCafe's (Yardi) report dated July 2, 2026, the average across professionally-managed apartment buildings — the type most common right around downtown — is $1,564/month for 787 square feet, with 1-bedrooms averaging $1,451.

Homes in our own portfolio in Central and Fairfax, the closest neighborhoods to campus where we manage rental homes, generally fall within our citywide range of about $700 to $1,800 a month, with most closer to $1,000.

Getting to and from Cleveland State without a car

Cleveland State sits directly on the HealthLine, GCRTA's bus-rapid-transit route running the length of Euclid Avenue between Public Square and University Circle, with continued service to East Cleveland. Tower City, the hub where the Red, Blue, and Green rail lines converge, is a short walk or ride away, giving direct connections toward Cleveland Hopkins Airport, Shaker Heights, and the east side.

  • Location: Downtown Cleveland, Euclid Avenue corridor near Public Square and Gateway
  • Transit: HealthLine (Euclid Avenue BRT); Tower City rail hub (Red/Blue/Green lines) nearby
  • Rent Finder Cleveland's nearest inventory: Central and Fairfax neighborhoods (ZIP 44104)
  • Distance to nearest inventory: A short ride southeast along Euclid or Chester Avenue

Section 8 and vouchers near Cleveland State

We don't currently manage units directly downtown, but every one of the 90+ rental homes we manage across Greater Cleveland — including our nearest homes in Central and Fairfax — accepts Housing Choice Vouchers (Section 8) and is HUD-inspection-ready. Ohio has no statewide law requiring a landlord to accept a voucher, and Cleveland does not currently have a citywide source-of-income protection ordinance, so acceptance at any specific downtown building should be confirmed with that building's owner.

Comparing downtown to nearby neighborhoods

Students and staff who don't need to be immediately adjacent to campus sometimes look east toward Central and Fairfax, or further into University Circle, for a wider range of housing types and price points. Fairfax sits directly adjacent to the Cleveland Clinic's main campus, and Central is the historic district just southeast of Downtown — both are a short ride from Cleveland State along Euclid or Chester Avenue rather than a walk.
