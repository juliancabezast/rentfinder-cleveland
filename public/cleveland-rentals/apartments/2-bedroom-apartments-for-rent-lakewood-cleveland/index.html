2-bedroom apartments in Lakewood, Ohio

Lakewood is one of the most densely populated communities in Cuyahoga County, and a large share of its residential buildings are pre-World War II apartment buildings and courtyard-style complexes concentrated along the Detroit Avenue and Clifton Boulevard corridors. Most of the city falls within ZIP code 44107, directly west of Cleveland's Cudell and Edgewater neighborhoods. In buildings like these, a 2-bedroom unit is typically the largest standard layout offered alongside studios and 1-bedrooms, and it's also the configuration most often found in Lakewood's two-family duplex houses on the side streets between the main corridors.

What do 2-bedroom apartments cost in Lakewood?

Lakewood-specific 2-bedroom pricing varies by building and corridor, but as a Greater Cleveland benchmark, Zumper's July 4, 2026 report shows a metro median 2-bedroom rent of $1,100/month. RentCafe (Yardi)'s July 2, 2026 report, which weights toward larger professionally managed apartment communities and averages roughly 1,027 square feet for a 2-bedroom, shows a considerably higher metro average of $1,818/month. Lakewood's mix of smaller pre-war walk-ups and larger managed apartment communities means an individual 2-bedroom listing could land anywhere across that range.

Getting around Lakewood without a car

Lakewood's density means many 2-bedroom apartment buildings sit within walking distance of the Detroit Avenue retail corridor, and GCRTA bus routes run along both Detroit Avenue and Clifton Boulevard into downtown Cleveland — an option a two-person household splitting a 2-bedroom may lean on to avoid paying for two parking spots. I-90 clips the southern edge of the city for anyone driving toward downtown or west toward Rocky River and Westlake, and Lakewood Park runs along the Lake Erie shoreline at the north end of town. For a household weighing a car-free lifestyle, the combination of dense bus service and a short drive or bus ride into downtown Cleveland is one of the more walkable, transit-served suburban options in the county.

  • ZIP code: 44107 (most of Lakewood)
  • Transit: RTA bus routes on Detroit Ave and Clifton Blvd; I-90 access
  • Location: Immediately west of Cleveland's Cudell and Edgewater neighborhoods, on Lake Erie
  • Housing stock: Pre-WWII apartment buildings and courtyard complexes with 2-bedroom units, plus two-family duplex houses

Section 8 vouchers for 2-bedroom apartments in Lakewood

Ohio has no statewide law requiring landlords to accept Housing Choice Vouchers, so acceptance for any given Lakewood 2-bedroom apartment comes down to the individual building owner rather than a citywide rule. What we can confirm about our own homes: every property Rent Finder Cleveland manages accepts Section 8 vouchers and is HUD-inspection-ready, though our current inventory sits in Cleveland proper, not Lakewood. The Cuyahoga Metropolitan Housing Authority (CMHA) administers the local voucher program countywide, including Lakewood, and accepts preliminary applications year-round.
