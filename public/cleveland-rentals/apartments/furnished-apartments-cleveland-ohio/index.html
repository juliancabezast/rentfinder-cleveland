Are there furnished apartments for rent in Cleveland?

Furnished apartments exist in Cleveland, but they're a smaller, separate slice of the rental market from standard year-long leases — most come through corporate-housing companies, extended-stay providers, or individual owners who furnish a unit specifically to serve traveling professionals, medical residents, or relocating employees on a short-term basis.

Furnished and short-term units typically carry a premium over an unfurnished, standard-lease apartment of the same size, since the rent has to cover furniture, utilities, and often a shorter or more flexible lease term. Furnished vs. short-term vs. standard lease

These three categories overlap but aren't identical. A furnished apartment can still come with a standard 12-month lease; a short-term rental can be unfurnished; and a corporate housing placement is typically both furnished and short-term, arranged through a company on behalf of an employee. If your priority is flexibility rather than furniture specifically, it's worth comparing all three before you search.

  • Furnished: Includes furniture and often kitchenware; lease length varies by provider
  • Short-term: Lease under 12 months; may or may not be furnished — see our short-term house rentals guide
  • Corporate housing: Typically furnished and short-term, arranged for a traveling employee — see our corporate housing guide
  • Standard lease: Unfurnished, 12-month term; the type of housing Rent Finder Cleveland works with

Who typically looks for a furnished apartment in Cleveland?

Furnished and short-term housing demand in Cleveland tends to come from people relocating for a defined period rather than settling permanently: employees relocating for a new job, medical professionals on a rotation at one of the region's major hospital systems, or graduate students and visiting faculty near University Circle. Cleveland Clinic and University Hospitals are the region's two largest employers, which keeps steady demand for short-term, furnished housing near the medical campuses on the east side.

Frequently asked questions

Does Rent Finder Cleveland offer furnished apartments?

How much does a furnished apartment cost in Cleveland?
There's no single Cleveland-wide furnished rate, but furnished and short-term units typically cost more per month than a comparable unfurnished lease. Zumper's July 4, 2026 report puts Cleveland's unfurnished 1-bedroom median at $1,195 and 2-bedroom at $1,100 — a useful floor to compare a furnished quote against.
Is a furnished apartment the same as corporate housing?
Not exactly. Corporate housing is typically both furnished and short-term, arranged through a company for a traveling or relocating employee. A furnished apartment can also be a standard 12-month lease offered directly by an owner, so it's worth asking about lease length separately from furnishing.
Are furnished apartments in Cleveland Section 8-friendly?
Furnished and short-term providers set their own voucher policies, which vary. Every standard, unfurnished rental home in Rent Finder Cleveland's own selection accepts Housing Choice Vouchers and is HUD-inspection-ready.
What's the difference between short-term and furnished rentals in Cleveland?
Short-term refers to lease length, typically under 12 months, and can be furnished or unfurnished. Furnished refers to whether furniture is included and can come with a lease of any length. The two often overlap but aren't the same thing.
