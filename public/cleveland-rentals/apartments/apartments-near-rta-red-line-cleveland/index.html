Which Cleveland apartments are actually near the Red Line?

The Red Line — known locally as the Rapid — runs one corridor from Cleveland Hopkins International Airport through Downtown's Tower City hub to Windermere Station in East Cleveland, so "near the Red Line" can mean very different parts of the city depending on which stop you mean. Our apartment-style rental homes are closest to the line's University Circle stop, since our Glenville and Hough inventory borders that area, and we also work with one home in the Ohio City ZIP (44113) near that station on the near-west side.

How much do apartments near the Red Line rent for?

Zumper's July 4, 2026 report puts Cleveland's citywide median asking rent at $1,195 for a 1-bedroom and $1,100 for a 2-bedroom, while RentCafe's (Yardi) July 2, 2026 report shows much higher averages — $1,451 and $1,818 respectively — for larger, professionally-managed apartment complexes. Neither source breaks out rent specifically by transit corridor.

Across our own selection near the University Circle and Ohio City stations, 2-bedroom apartment-style homes generally run from about $750 to $1,100 a month, and 3-bedroom homes from about $1,100 to $1,500, reflecting the older duplex and triplex stock most of our inventory sits in rather than large new-construction buildings.

Living car-optional near Cleveland's rail network

Tower City isn't just a stop on the Red Line — it's where the Blue and Green light-rail lines (both running to Shaker Heights) and the Waterfront Line (to the Flats and North Coast lakefront) all converge, so a home near any of those connecting lines still puts you within one transfer of the Red Line corridor. The HealthLine, RTA's bus-rapid-transit route along Euclid Avenue, runs a separate but connected corridor between Public Square and University Circle — useful if your daily trip runs toward Cleveland Clinic, University Hospitals, or Case Western Reserve University, three of the region's largest employers.

If a car-optional commute matters, ask us specifically about a station or the HealthLine route rather than just "near the Rapid" — a short walk to a stop makes a bigger practical difference than being generally on the same side of the city.

Frequently asked questions

How often does the Red Line run?
Trains run roughly every 15 minutes throughout the day. The Red Line, known locally as the Rapid, connects Cleveland Hopkins International Airport to Downtown's Tower City hub and continues to Windermere Station in East Cleveland, with a stop at University Circle in between.
Do you have apartments near the University Circle Red Line stop?
Our closest apartment-style inventory is in Glenville and Hough, both of which border University Circle. Availability changes, so book a showing and tell us your target commute and we'll match you with current options near that stop.
How much does an apartment near the Red Line cost?
Across our available homes near the University Circle and Ohio City stations, 2-bedroom apartment-style homes typically run about $750 to $1,100 a month and 3-bedrooms about $1,100 to $1,500. Citywide, Zumper's July 4, 2026 report lists a $1,100 median for 2-bedrooms, while RentCafe's July 2, 2026 report shows a higher average for large managed complexes.
Can I commute to Cleveland Clinic or University Hospitals without a car?
Yes. The Red Line's University Circle stop and RTA's HealthLine bus-rapid-transit route along Euclid Avenue both serve that area directly, connecting to Cleveland Clinic, University Hospitals, and Case Western Reserve University.
Can I use a Section 8 voucher on an apartment near the Red Line?
Yes. Every apartment-style home we work with accepts Housing Choice Vouchers and is HUD-inspection-ready, regardless of distance from any Red Line station. Check our Section 8 guide for help with the local CMHA application process.
