Apartment rents in Lakewood, Ohio

Lakewood sits directly on Lake Erie, immediately west of Cleveland's West Side, sharing a border with Cleveland's lakefront neighborhoods. It's one of Cuyahoga County's West Side suburbs, and its housing stock leans toward older apartment buildings and converted multi-family homes rather than new-construction complexes.

There's no independently published Lakewood-only rent index, so the closest reliable benchmark for renters comparing prices is the broader Greater Cleveland metro-area data below. Actual Lakewood pricing depends heavily on the specific building, unit condition, and whether it's a classic pre-war walk-up or a newer conversion.

Lakewood's location and nearby Cleveland neighborhoods

Lakewood borders Cleveland's West Side lakefront neighborhoods — Cudell and Edgewater — both of which sit along Detroit Avenue and the Lake Erie shoreline, with Edgewater home to Edgewater Park, part of the Cleveland Metroparks Lakefront system. Because Lakewood sits on Cleveland's west side of the Cuyahoga River, it typically sees less lake-effect snowfall each winter than communities on the city's east side.

GCRTA, the regional transit authority, runs bus service throughout Lakewood connecting to Downtown Cleveland and the Tower City rail hub, where the Red, Blue, Green, and Waterfront lines converge.

Section 8 and voucher-friendly apartments near Lakewood

Ohio has no statewide source-of-income protection law, so whether a landlord accepts a Housing Choice Voucher depends on local ordinance or the individual owner's policy. As of July 2026, the reported Cuyahoga County suburbs with a local voucher-holder protection ordinance are Cleveland Heights, South Euclid, University Heights, Warrensville Heights, and Linndale — Lakewood is not currently on that list, so acceptance there depends on the individual landlord; renters should verify directly.

Every rental home Rent Finder Cleveland manages accepts Housing Choice Vouchers and is HUD-inspection-ready, including our homes near Lakewood in the 44102 ZIP. Vouchers in Cuyahoga County are administered by the Cuyahoga Metropolitan Housing Authority (CMHA); our Section 8 housing guide covers applying, waitlists, and inspections.

Tips for finding a cheap apartment near Lakewood

When comparing prices, check both Zumper's median-asking figures and RentCafe's professionally-managed averages — Zumper's own reporting notes that 2-bedroom asking rents can sometimes sit at or below 1-bedroom rents simply because of which units happen to be listed that month, not a pricing error. Widening your search a few blocks east into Cleveland's Cudell and Detroit-Shoreway neighborhoods can open up more voucher-friendly options at similar price points.

If a Housing Choice Voucher is part of your plan, start the CMHA application in parallel with your apartment search — preliminary intake runs year-round with no closing date, but placement is by random lottery and can take anywhere from a few days to several months.
