Where is Case Western Reserve University, exactly?

Case Western Reserve University is located in University Circle, one of Cleveland's 34 officially designated Statistical Planning Areas on the city's east side. University Circle is also home to the Cleveland Museum of Art, Severance Music Center, and sits near the Cleveland Clinic's main campus — making it one of the city's denser institutional and residential districts. The area is directly served by the RTA Red Line (Cleveland's heavy-rail line) and the HealthLine, a bus-rapid-transit route running along Euclid Avenue between Public Square downtown and University Circle/East Cleveland.

Apartment inventory directly inside University Circle tends to be dominated by large purpose-built student and institutional housing. Renters who want more housing options, including standard rental homes, often look just outside the core campus footprint in adjacent east-side neighborhoods along the same transit corridor.

Getting to and from campus without a car

The HealthLine runs in a dedicated center-median busway along Euclid Avenue, connecting Public Square, the Midtown corridor, University Circle, and East Cleveland — making it one of the more direct ways to reach campus from east-side neighborhoods without driving. The RTA Red Line, Cleveland's only heavy-rail line, also runs through the east side toward Windermere in East Cleveland, with all rail lines converging at Tower City downtown, where riders can transfer to the Blue and Green light-rail lines toward Shaker Heights. Renters weighing a neighborhood near campus should check its specific bus routes and walking distance to the nearest HealthLine or Red Line stop, since transit access can vary block to block even within the same general area.

Beyond rail and the HealthLine, GCRTA also runs a broader network of local bus routes and paratransit service across Cuyahoga County, so a neighborhood a few miles from campus can still be a reasonable commute if it sits along one of these lines. Anyone relying on transit rather than a car should confirm current routes and schedules directly with GCRTA before signing a lease, since service can change.
