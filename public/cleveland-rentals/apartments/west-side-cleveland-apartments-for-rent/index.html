What counts as Cleveland's "west side"?

Cleveland's neighborhoods are officially organized into 34 Statistical Planning Areas by the City Planning Commission, split generally by position relative to the Cuyahoga River. The west side covers 14 of those areas, including Ohio City (home to the West Side Market and the RTA Red Line's Ohio City station), Tremont (an arts and dining district near Lincoln Park), Detroit-Shoreway (home to the Gordon Square Arts District), Edgewater (lakefront, near Edgewater Park), Clark-Fulton (along the MetroHealth main campus corridor), Old Brooklyn (near the Cleveland Metroparks Zoo), and Kamm's Corners and Jefferson in the far-west West Park area near Cleveland Hopkins International Airport.

Renters searching "west side Cleveland apartments" are typically comparing this stretch of neighborhoods against the city's east side, which runs from Downtown through University Circle, Hough, Glenville, and out toward the eastern suburbs. The west side's housing stock leans toward early-1900s single-family homes, duplexes, and small apartment buildings, with denser mid-rise construction concentrated closer to Ohio City, Tremont, and the Detroit-Shoreway/Gordon Square corridor.

What does rent look like on the west side?

West-side rents vary widely by neighborhood and building age. Getting around the west side

The RTA Red Line, Cleveland's heavy-rail line, runs from Cleveland Hopkins International Airport through the near-west side (including an Ohio City station) to Tower City downtown and on to the east side — Cleveland's only rapid-transit line that directly connects downtown to the city's main airport. West-side neighborhoods farther from the rail line, such as Kamm's Corners and Jefferson in the West Park area, rely more heavily on GCRTA's local bus network. I-90, I-71, and I-480 all run through or near west-side neighborhoods, giving drivers direct highway access toward downtown and the outer suburbs. Renters without a car should check the specific bus routes serving any west-side address before committing to a lease, since coverage and frequency vary by neighborhood.
