Tri-C's four campuses and how commutes differ

Because Tri-C's footprint spans the city and several suburbs, "near Tri-C" means something different depending on your campus:

  • Metropolitan Campus (Metro): located just southeast of Downtown Cleveland, the closest of the four campuses to the city's core and its Downtown/University Circle neighborhoods
  • Western Campus: located in Parma, southwest of Cleveland, closer to the city's west-side and southwest suburbs
  • Eastern Campus: located in Highland Hills, southeast of Cleveland near the eastern suburbs
  • Westshore Campus: located near Westlake, on the far west side along the Lake Erie corridor

How much does it cost to rent a house near Tri-C?

Across our current Cleveland-area portfolio, rent generally runs from about $700 to $1,800 a month, with most homes landing near $1,000 — a range that fits a lot of student and staff budgets whether you're commuting to Metro, Western, Eastern, or Westshore. Two- and three-bedroom homes are the most common sizes we manage, which also works well for students splitting rent with roommates.

For broader market context, Zumper's report dated July 4, 2026 put Cleveland's median asking rent at $1,250 a month, and noted Cleveland runs about 35.9% below the $1,950 national median — a meaningful affordability edge if you're commuting to any of Tri-C's campuses on a student or staff budget.

Getting to campus without a car

If Metro Campus is your target, its location just southeast of Downtown puts it within reach of Greater Cleveland RTA's rail hub at Tower City, where the Red Line, Blue Line, Green Line, and Waterfront Line all converge, plus an extensive bus network across the city. The other three campuses — Western in Parma, Eastern in Highland Hills, and Westshore near Westlake — sit outside the immediate downtown transit hub, so a car or a longer bus commute typically factors more into the trip.

If your household doesn't have reliable transportation, ask us about listings closer to Downtown or an RTA line before committing to a specific address — commute time matters more for a daily class schedule than it does for most other renters.

Section 8 vouchers for Tri-C students and staff

Every home we manage accepts Housing Choice Vouchers (Section 8) and is kept HUD-inspection-ready, whether you're a student, staff member, or unrelated to Tri-C entirely. Voucher eligibility itself is set by the Cuyahoga Metropolitan Housing Authority (CMHA), not by us, so if you don't already have a voucher, our Cleveland Section 8 guide explains how the local application and waitlist process works.

Frequently asked questions

Which Tri-C campus is closest to Downtown Cleveland?
Tri-C's Metropolitan Campus (Metro) sits just southeast of Downtown Cleveland, making it the closest of the college's four campuses to the city center and to RTA's Tower City rail hub. The other three campuses — Western, Eastern, and Westshore — are in Parma, Highland Hills, and near Westlake.
Do you have houses near every Tri-C campus?
We manage rental homes across Greater Cleveland, concentrated on the city's East and Southeast side, so how close a listing is to a specific Tri-C campus depends on which campus and which home. Tell us your campus and commute needs when you book a showing so we can point you to the closest current fit.
Can I split rent with roommates while attending Tri-C?
Yes. Many of our two- and three-bedroom houses work well for roommates splitting rent, and rent for those sizes typically runs from about $750 to $1,400 a month. Ask about a specific listing's layout and lease terms before applying with roommates.
Is there public transit near Tri-C's campuses?
It depends on the campus. Metro Campus, near Downtown, is within reach of RTA's Tower City rail hub (Red, Blue, Green, and Waterfront lines) and city bus routes. Western, Eastern, and Westshore campuses sit outside the immediate downtown transit network, so a car or longer bus commute is more common.
Can I use a Section 8 voucher while attending Tri-C?
Yes — every home we manage accepts Housing Choice Vouchers and is HUD-inspection-ready. Voucher eligibility and issuance are handled by CMHA, not by Tri-C or by us, so check our Section 8 guide if you don't already have a voucher and want to understand the local process.
