2 Bedroom Houses for Rent in Cleveland, OH

2 bedroom houses are the most common rental size in Cleveland, OH, typically renting for $750 to $1,100 a month. They're widely available across the city's East and Southeast side neighborhoods, and most local 2-bedroom rental homes accept Housing Choice Vouchers (Section 8).

What does a 2 bedroom house rent for in Cleveland?

A 2-bedroom house in Cleveland typically rents for $750 to $1,100 a month, depending on condition, location, and whether utilities are included. Two-bedroom homes make up the single largest share of our rental portfolio — more than any other bedroom count — so this is usually the size with the most current options across the city.

For context, Zumper's July 4, 2026 report puts Cleveland's citywide median 2-bedroom asking rent at $1,100/mo, in line with what we typically see across our own 2-bedroom homes. RentCafe's July 2, 2026 report on professionally-managed apartment complexes shows a 2-bedroom apartment averaging $1,818/mo — much higher, since that figure reflects newer, amenity-heavy apartment buildings rather than the older rental houses and duplex units that make up most of Cleveland's 2-bedroom house inventory.

Where are 2 bedroom houses available in Cleveland?

Two-bedroom homes are common across our East and Southeast side inventory — Slavic Village (44105), Collinwood (44110), Glenville (44108), Fairfax/Central (44104), Hough (44103), and Buckeye-Shaker (44120) — as well as a smaller number on the West side around Cudell/Detroit-Shoreway (44102) and Old Brooklyn (44109). GCRTA's bus network reaches all of these areas, and the Blue and Green light-rail lines serve the Buckeye-Shaker corridor directly into downtown's Tower City hub.

What's included in a Cleveland 2 bedroom rental?

Most of our 2-bedroom homes are one full unit within a duplex or similar small multi-family building, though some are true single-family houses. Expect a full basement in most cases (often used for laundry hookups and storage), and a small-to-medium yard. Utility arrangements vary by property — some include water or trash, most do not include electric or gas — so always confirm exactly what's covered before signing.

Two-bedroom layouts also vary: some are one-bedroom-plus-den style with a smaller second room, while others have two comparably sized bedrooms. If you're touring a duplex unit, ask whether you'd share the basement or yard with the other unit's tenant, since that arrangement is common in Cleveland's older two- and three-family buildings and can affect day-to-day living more than the square footage alone.

Section 8 and 2 bedroom vouchers

Every 2-bedroom home we manage accepts Housing Choice Vouchers and is HUD-inspection-ready. Voucher holders in Cuyahoga County work through the Cuyahoga Metropolitan Housing Authority (CMHA), which sets the payment standard — the maximum monthly subsidy — for each bedroom size. CMHA's payment standards change periodically, so check the current chart directly with CMHA rather than relying on a fixed number here. See HUD's overview of the Housing Choice Voucher program for how the program works nationally, or read our Section 8 housing guide for Cleveland-specific detail.
