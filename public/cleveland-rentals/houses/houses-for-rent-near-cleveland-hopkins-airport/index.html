Houses for rent near the airport (Hopkins)

Cleveland Hopkins International Airport sits in the far southwest corner of Cleveland's Hopkins planning area, directly on GCRTA's Red Line, which runs straight through downtown. Nearby communities include Brook Park and Berea. Rent Finder Cleveland works with Section 8-friendly rental homes across greater Cleveland, including some on the west side closer to this corridor.

Where Cleveland Hopkins Airport sits

Cleveland Hopkins International Airport occupies Hopkins, one of Cleveland's 34 official Statistical Planning Areas, in the far southwest corner of the city near the I-71 corridor. The neighboring Bellaire-Puritas planning area, just to the north, also runs along that same I-71/airport corridor.

Just outside the city line, Berea (home to Baldwin Wallace University) and Brook Park border the airport property, giving renters who work at or near Hopkins several nearby municipalities to consider beyond Cleveland's city limits.

Renters searching for a house in this corridor are effectively choosing between Cleveland's own Hopkins and Bellaire-Puritas neighborhoods and the surrounding suburbs, since the airport property itself sits at the edge of the city rather than in the middle of dense residential blocks. That makes transit access, rather than a single named district, the more useful way to think about "airport-area" renting.

Getting downtown from the airport area

GCRTA's Red Line is a heavy-rail route running from the airport ("Airport–Windermere") straight through downtown's Tower City to Windermere station in East Cleveland, with trains roughly every 15 minutes throughout the day. It's the only rapid-transit line in the country that connects directly to its city's main airport terminal, which makes it a genuine car-free commute option for airport-area workers, not just travelers.

What do rental houses cost in southwest Cleveland?

Cleveland's citywide median asking rent was $1,250 a month per Zumper's July 4, 2026 report, with 3-bedroom houses running closer to $1,350 — a useful benchmark for the larger homes airport-area families typically look for. Because houses in this corridor tend to be older and smaller than the newer complexes RentCafe's dataset leans on, expect prices nearer Zumper's figures than RentCafe's July 2, 2026 average of $1,564 for professionally managed apartments.

Zumper's 2-bedroom figure of $1,100 is also a reasonable starting point for airport-corridor houses, since two-bedroom homes make up the largest share of Cleveland's rental-house stock overall. Actual prices in Brook Park, Berea, and Cleveland's own southwest neighborhoods will vary with lot size, age, and whether a garage is included, so treat these citywide numbers as a planning benchmark rather than a quote for any specific address.

Section 8 vouchers near the airport

Every rental home we work with, regardless of neighborhood, accepts Housing Choice Vouchers (Section 8) and is HUD-inspection-ready. The Cuyahoga Metropolitan Housing Authority (CMHA) runs the county's voucher program, but voucher protections vary by city: Ohio has no statewide source-of-income law, and Cleveland proper doesn't ban source-of-income discrimination, so ask any landlord in Brook Park or Berea directly whether they accept a voucher.
