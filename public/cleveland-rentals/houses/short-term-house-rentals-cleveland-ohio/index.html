Short Term House Rentals in Cleveland, Ohio

True short-term house rentals — leases under six months — are limited in Cleveland's private rental market. Rent Finder Cleveland's 90+ rental homes are typically leased on standard 12-month terms; renters needing a shorter stay should ask directly about flexibility, or look at corporate and month-to-month housing providers built specifically for temporary needs.

Updated July 4, 2026 ·4 min read ·By the Rent Finder Cleveland team

Are short-term house rentals available in Cleveland?

Short-term is a spectrum: some renters mean a few weeks, others mean a 6-to-9-month lease instead of a full year. In practice, most Cleveland landlords — including Rent Finder Cleveland — write standard 12-month leases for houses and duplexes, since that's what covers turnover costs and gives both sides predictability. We manage 90+ rental homes across Greater Cleveland, concentrated on the East and Southeast side, and every one accepts Section 8 Housing Choice Vouchers and is HUD-inspection-ready.

That said, timelines do shift unit by unit — a home coming off a lease, a listing sitting longer than expected, or a landlord open to a shorter first term. The only reliable way to find out is to book a showing and tell our team your exact timeframe.

What's the difference between short-term, month-to-month, and standard leases?

A standard lease runs a fixed term, usually 12 months, with a set end date. A month-to-month lease renews automatically each month until either side gives notice, and can follow an initial fixed term or start that way from day one. Short-term or corporate housing usually means a lease of a few weeks to a few months, often furnished, and priced at a premium to cover the flexibility. Our guide to month-to-month leases in Ohio covers how that arrangement works and what to expect if you need to end it, and our guide on breaking a lease in Ohio explains the general risks of leaving a fixed-term lease early.

  • Standard lease: fixed 12-month term, most common for our houses and duplexes
  • Month-to-month: renews monthly, more flexible, less common for standalone houses
  • Short-term / corporate: weeks to a few months, often furnished, priced at a premium

What does a Cleveland house rental cost, short-term or standard?

Standard-lease houses in our Cleveland portfolio typically run $750–$1,100 a month for two bedrooms and $1,000–$1,500 for three bedrooms. Short-term and corporate arrangements are priced individually by the provider and generally cost more per month than an equivalent standard lease, since the rate has to cover vacancy risk between short stays. For citywide context, Zumper's rent report dated July 4, 2026 put Cleveland's overall median asking rent at $1,250/mo, and RentCafe's report dated July 2, 2026 put the professionally-managed average at $1,564/mo.

Common situations include job relocations still finalizing a permanent address, home purchases or renovations in progress, insurance-related displacement, and traveling professionals on temporary assignment. Section 8 and short-term leases

Voucher holders working with the Cuyahoga Metropolitan Housing Authority (CMHA) should plan around a standard lease term rather than a short-term stay, since a voucher unit still has to pass CMHA's housing quality inspection and get set up in the program before assistance starts. Frequently asked questions

Can I rent a house in Cleveland for less than a year?
It's possible but limited — most houses in our portfolio and across the Cleveland market are leased on standard 12-month terms. Some landlords will consider a shorter first term depending on the unit and timing. The fastest way to find out is to book a showing and share your exact timeframe.
Is a month-to-month lease the same as a short-term rental?
Not exactly. A month-to-month lease renews automatically each month and can run indefinitely, while a short-term or corporate rental is usually a fixed few weeks to a few months, often furnished. Both offer more flexibility than a standard 12-month lease, but they work differently.
Does a short-term rental cost more than a standard lease in Cleveland?
Generally yes. Short-term and corporate rentals are priced by the provider to cover vacancy risk between shorter stays, so the monthly rate typically runs higher than an equivalent standard 12-month lease for the same size home.
Can I use a Section 8 voucher for a short-term rental?
Voucher assistance is built around a standard lease term and CMHA's inspection process, so it fits a standard lease better than a short-term stay. Every home we manage accepts Section 8 and is HUD-inspection-ready, and our leasing team can walk through timing if you hold a voucher.
