Why Cleveland's rental housing stock varies so much in age

Cleveland's neighborhoods were largely built out in the early-to-mid 1900s, so much of the city's rental housing — including many homes we work with in Slavic Village (44105), Collinwood (44110), Glenville (44108), and Hough (44103) — predates 1978. Under the City of Cleveland's Lead-Safe Certification program, rental units built before January 1, 1978 are presumed to contain lead-based paint and must pass a lead-safe inspection to be certified. The program began in 2019, carried a compliance deadline of March 1, 2023, and certification is valid for two years before a property must be re-evaluated.

That's part of why "renovated" means different things on different blocks: a recently updated kitchen doesn't always mean the whole structure has been brought up to current standards, and vice versa. Ask about both cosmetic updates and lead-safe certification status for any specific address you're considering, especially in older sections of the city where original construction predates the 1978 threshold by several decades.

What kind of updates do renters typically find?

Updates we see across our Cleveland selection typically fall into a few categories — none guaranteed on every home, but common enough to ask about before you tour.

  • Kitchens and bathrooms: updated cabinets, counters, or fixtures on select listings
  • Flooring: replaced flooring in higher-turnover units
  • Systems: furnace, water heater, or electrical updates as properties turn over
  • Exterior: roofing and siding work that varies by building age

How renovation affects rent

Rent in our current Cleveland listings runs from about $750 to $1,100 a month for two-bedroom homes and $1,100 to $1,800 for three- and four-bedroom homes, and a recently updated unit tends to sit at the higher end of its size range rather than command an entirely different price bracket.

For comparison, RentCafe's report dated July 2, 2026 placed Cleveland's average rent for professionally managed apartments at $1,564 a month — higher than most of our listings, since that figure reflects larger managed apartment complexes rather than the individual houses and small multi-family buildings that make up our available homes.

Frequently asked questions

Are all your Cleveland rental homes newly renovated?
No — renovation status varies by property. Our Cleveland selection includes homes at different stages of updates, from recently renovated kitchens to homes that haven't had cosmetic work in years. Ask about a specific address's condition when you book a showing.
Does a renovated home cost more to rent?
Usually only modestly. Rent is driven mainly by bedroom count and location; an updated unit tends to land at the higher end of its size range — for example, closer to $1,100 for a two-bedroom — rather than a different price bracket entirely.
What is Cleveland's Lead-Safe Certification, and does it apply to renovated homes?
It's a city program requiring rental units built before January 1, 1978 to pass a lead-safe inspection, regardless of recent cosmetic updates. The program began in 2019 with a 2023 compliance deadline, and certification is valid for two years before re-evaluation.
Do renovated homes still accept Section 8 vouchers?
Yes. Every home we work with — recently updated or not — accepts Housing Choice Vouchers and is kept HUD-inspection-ready, which is a separate standard from cosmetic renovation.
How do I find out if a specific listing has been updated?
Book a free showing and ask our leasing team directly. We'll tell you what's actually been done to a specific property — kitchen, flooring, systems, or otherwise — rather than market it as generically "renovated."
