What counts as an affordable house in Cleveland?

Cleveland is already one of the more affordable rental markets in the country. Per Zumper's July 4, 2026 report, Cleveland's median asking rent is $1,250/mo — about 35.9% below the U.S. national median of $1,950/mo. Within that market, "affordable" for a house generally means a 2- or 3-bedroom home priced from the high-$600s to roughly $1,250 a month.

Which Cleveland neighborhoods tend to be most affordable?

The lowest house rents tend to cluster in Cleveland's East and Southeast side neighborhoods, where much of Rent Finder Cleveland's inventory sits: Slavic Village (44105), Collinwood (44110), Glenville (44108), Fairfax and Central (44104), Hough (44103), and Buckeye-Shaker (44120). We also manage a smaller number of homes on the West side, including Cudell/Detroit-Shoreway (44102) and Old Brooklyn (44109). These neighborhoods are covered by multiple GCRTA bus routes, and Buckeye-Shaker sits directly on the RTA Blue/Green light-rail corridor along Shaker Boulevard.

Section 8 and other ways to stretch an affordable budget

For renters using a Housing Choice Voucher, affordability is measured differently — the voucher covers a portion of rent up to CMHA's payment standard for the bedroom size, with the household paying the remainder based on income. Every home Rent Finder Cleveland manages accepts Section 8 vouchers and is HUD-inspection-ready. Note that Ohio has no statewide source-of-income protection law and Cleveland itself has not enacted one, so voucher acceptance still varies by individual landlord outside of a few suburbs (Cleveland Heights, South Euclid, University Heights, Warrensville Heights, Linndale) with local protections.

Read more in our Cleveland Section 8 guide, or check current CMHA income limits directly at cmha.net.

What else affects total affordability beyond rent?

Rent is the largest line item, but a realistic budget also includes utilities and a security deposit. Cleveland electric service comes from either Cleveland Public Power or The Illuminating Company depending on the address, natural gas is mostly Enbridge Gas Ohio, and water is billed through Cleveland Water — worth confirming per address since providers vary by block. Winter heating costs run higher than average given Cleveland's lake-effect snow belt climate (roughly 60–70 inches of snow a season), so budget for a seasonal bump in gas or electric bills from November through March.

For move-in costs, Ohio law also governs how a landlord must handle your security deposit, including interest requirements and the timeline for returning it after move-out — see our Ohio security deposit guide for specifics.

Frequently asked questions

What is considered an affordable rent in Cleveland, Ohio?
Cleveland's median asking rent was $1,250/mo as of Zumper's July 2026 report, about 36% below the U.S. national median. For houses specifically, affordable generally means roughly $700 to $1,250 a month for a 2- or 3-bedroom home, depending on neighborhood and condition.
Where are the cheapest houses for rent in Cleveland?
The most affordable house rents tend to be on Cleveland's East and Southeast sides, in neighborhoods like Slavic Village, Collinwood, Glenville, Fairfax/Central, Hough, and Buckeye-Shaker, where much of our own rental portfolio is located.
Does Rent Finder Cleveland manage low-cost rental houses?
Yes. We manage 90+ rental homes across Greater Cleveland with rents typically ranging from about $700 to $1,800 a month, and a large share fall well within an affordable, budget-conscious range. Every home accepts Section 8 vouchers.
Can I use a Section 8 voucher on an affordable house?
Yes, on any home we manage — 100% of our portfolio accepts Housing Choice Vouchers and is HUD-inspection-ready. Confirm your specific payment standard with CMHA, since the voucher amount depends on bedroom size and household income.
Are affordable houses harder to get approved for?
Not inherently — screening (income, ID, rental history) applies at every price point. A lower rent can actually make the standard income-to-rent ratio easier to meet. See our guide on the rental application process for what to prepare.
