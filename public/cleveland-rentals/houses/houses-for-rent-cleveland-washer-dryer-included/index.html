Do Cleveland rental houses come with a washer and dryer?

It varies by property, but in-home laundry is a realistic feature to look for in Cleveland's house rental stock. Because most of the city's older single-family and duplex homes have a full basement, that basement is the most common location for laundry hookups or an included washer and dryer, rather than a dedicated laundry closet or room.

What laundry setups are common in Cleveland houses?

Because laundry setup differs property to property, it's worth understanding the range of what's typical before you tour, so you know exactly what to ask about.

  • Washer/dryer included: Machines are already in the unit, most often in the basement
  • Hookups only: Basement plumbing and electrical are in place, but you supply your own machines
  • Shared laundry: More common in multi-unit duplex/triplex buildings than in single-family houses
  • No laundry on site: Some older homes have neither hookups nor machines, requiring a laundromat

What does rent look like for houses with laundry included?

Rent for a house with laundry included follows the same market factors as any Cleveland rental — bedroom count, condition, and neighborhood — rather than a separate laundry premium. Per Zumper's July 4, 2026 report, Cleveland's citywide median asking rent is $1,250/mo, with 2-bedroom homes averaging around $1,100 and 3-bedroom homes around $1,350.

Where are washer/dryer homes typically found in Cleveland?

Full basements — and the laundry hookups that go with them — are typical across Cleveland's East and Southeast side neighborhoods, where most of Rent Finder Cleveland's managed inventory sits, including Slavic Village (44105), Collinwood (44110), Glenville (44108), Fairfax and Central (44104), Hough (44103), and Buckeye-Shaker (44120) — plus a smaller number of West-side homes near Cudell/Detroit-Shoreway (44102) and Old Brooklyn (44109). These are largely early-20th-century houses and duplexes built with full basements as standard construction, which is why basement laundry is the norm rather than a dedicated laundry room.

Do washer/dryer homes accept Section 8 vouchers?

Every home Rent Finder Cleveland manages — with or without in-unit laundry — accepts Housing Choice Vouchers (Section 8) and is HUD-inspection-ready. Voucher holders should know that Ohio has no statewide source-of-income protection law and the City of Cleveland does not require landlords to accept vouchers; only a handful of suburbs (Cleveland Heights, South Euclid, University Heights, Warrensville Heights, and Linndale) have local ordinances protecting voucher holders. Learn more on the CMHA Housing Choice Voucher page or our Cleveland Section 8 guide.

Frequently asked questions

Are there houses for rent in Cleveland with washer and dryer included?
Yes, a number of Cleveland rental houses include a washer and dryer, most commonly set up in the basement since full basements are standard in the city's older housing stock. Availability varies by property, so ask specifically about laundry setup when you book a showing.
Is laundry usually in the basement in Cleveland rental houses?
Yes. Most of Cleveland's single-family houses and duplexes were built with full basements, and that's typically where laundry hookups or machines are located, rather than a dedicated laundry room elsewhere in the home.
What's the difference between washer/dryer included and hookups only?
"Included" means the machines are already in the unit and ready to use. "Hookups only" means the plumbing and electrical connections exist in the basement, but the tenant needs to bring or buy their own washer and dryer. Always confirm which applies before signing a lease.
Do houses with laundry included cost more to rent in Cleveland?
Not necessarily. Rent is driven mainly by bedroom count, condition, and neighborhood rather than laundry setup specifically. Since basement laundry hookups are common in Cleveland's housing stock, they don't typically carry a large separate price premium.
Do washer/dryer houses accept Section 8 vouchers?
Yes. Every home Rent Finder Cleveland manages accepts Housing Choice Vouchers and is HUD-inspection-ready, regardless of laundry setup. Voucher holders should confirm current CMHA rules and know that source-of-income protections only apply in a few Cuyahoga County suburbs, not the City of Cleveland itself.
