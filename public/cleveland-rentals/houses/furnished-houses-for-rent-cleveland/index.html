Can you rent a furnished house in Cleveland?

Genuinely furnished single-family houses are rare in Cleveland's everyday rental market. Most landlords — Rent Finder Cleveland included — lease houses and duplexes unfurnished on standard 12-month terms, so tenants bring their own furniture. We manage 90+ rental homes across Greater Cleveland, concentrated on the city's East and Southeast side in neighborhoods such as Slavic Village, Collinwood, Glenville, and Hough, and every home we manage accepts Section 8 Housing Choice Vouchers and is HUD-inspection-ready.

If a fully furnished house is a hard requirement, it's usually faster to look at furnished apartments or dedicated corporate housing providers, which more commonly stock move-in-ready furniture on shorter terms. If some flexibility is possible, book a showing and ask our leasing team directly about a unit's condition and move-in timeline.

Who actually needs a furnished rental in Cleveland?

Furnished-house demand tends to come from a narrow set of situations rather than the general rental market: traveling healthcare workers on short assignments near Cleveland's hospital systems, corporate relocations, insurance-related displacement after a fire or flood, and out-of-state buyers bridging a home purchase. Everyday long-term renters make up the bulk of demand for our unfurnished portfolio instead.

If a short assignment or temporary stay is the goal, our travel nurse housing guide and short-term rental guide walk through what's realistic and where to start looking.

How much does it cost to rent a house in Cleveland, furnished or not?

Unfurnished houses in our portfolio typically run $750–$1,100 a month for two bedrooms and $1,000–$1,500 for three bedrooms, within a citywide range of roughly $700 to $1,800. Cleveland's broader market runs similarly: Zumper's rent report dated July 4, 2026 put the citywide median asking rent at $1,250/mo (three-bedroom median $1,350), while RentCafe's report dated July 2, 2026 — which indexes larger, professionally managed buildings — put the average at $1,564/mo. Furnished and corporate-style rentals generally carry a premium over these unfurnished baselines, since furniture, utilities, and shorter lease flexibility are typically built into the monthly rate.

Do Section 8 vouchers work with furnished housing?

A home's furnishing status doesn't change its Section 8 eligibility — Housing Choice Voucher inspections evaluate the safety and condition of the unit itself, not whether it comes with furniture. Every home Rent Finder Cleveland manages already accepts vouchers and is kept HUD-inspection-ready under HUD's Housing Choice Voucher program standards. Renters using a voucher for one of our unfurnished houses should plan to supply their own furniture, since CMHA's inspection process focuses on habitability, not furnishings. Can I use a Section 8 voucher on a furnished rental?
A voucher can be used on any unit that passes CMHA's inspection and meets program rules, regardless of furnishing. Since furnished houses are uncommon in Cleveland's private rental market, most voucher holders rent unfurnished homes — all of ours accept Section 8 and are HUD-inspection-ready.
