2 Bedroom houses for rent in Cleveland under $1000

A $1,000-a-month budget covers most of the 2-bedroom rental homes in Rent Finder Cleveland's selection, which generally price between $750 and $1,100. That figure also lines up closely with Cleveland's own citywide median rent, and every 2-bedroom home we work with accepts Section 8 Housing Choice Vouchers.

Updated July 4, 2026 ·4 min read ·By the Rent Finder Cleveland team

How far does $1,000 a month go for a 2-bedroom house in Cleveland?

In our own selection, 2-bedroom rental homes generally price between about $750 and $1,100 a month, so a $1,000 budget covers the large majority of what we currently work with. That figure is also close to the roughly $1,000 median rent across our full Cleveland selection of all home sizes, making it a realistic, market-anchored target rather than a bargain-basement number.

Zumper's July 4, 2026 report puts Cleveland's citywide median 2-bedroom asking rent at $1,100, meaning a $1,000 cap keeps you at or slightly under the typical market rate for a 2-bedroom unit across the whole city, not just within our listings.

Because two-bedroom homes are the single largest category in our Cleveland selection, a $1,000 budget also tends to open up more choice of neighborhood, home age, and condition than the same budget would for a 3- or 4-bedroom home, where fewer options exist below that price.

Which neighborhoods fit a $1,000 2-bedroom budget?

A $1,000 ceiling opens up more of Cleveland's east and southeast side than a stricter sub-$900 search would, including a few neighborhoods where our 2-bedroom homes run slightly higher than the cheapest end of our available homes.

  • Fairfax/Central (ZIP 44104) — east side, adjacent to the Cleveland Clinic main campus
  • Hough (ZIP 44103) — east side, between downtown and University Circle, served by the HealthLine on Euclid Avenue
  • Buckeye-Shaker (ZIP 44120) — southeast side, along the RTA Blue/Green light-rail corridor bordering Shaker Heights
  • Slavic Village (ZIP 44105) and Collinwood (ZIP 44110) — our two largest concentrations, where many 2-bedroom homes also fall comfortably under $1,000

How does $1,000 compare across bedroom sizes?

A $1,000 cap is roomier for 2-bedroom homes than for 3-bedroom homes, which more commonly start above $1,100 in our available homes. RentCafe's July 2, 2026 report shows just how wide the spread can get across the broader Cleveland apartment market, where a 2-bedroom averages $1,818 and a 3-bedroom averages $2,716 — both figures skewed by newer, larger managed complexes rather than the older rental houses that make up most of our inventory.

Section 8 and vouchers under a $1,000 budget

Every 2-bedroom home we work with accepts Housing Choice Vouchers (Section 8) and is HUD-inspection-ready, regardless of whether the rent lands closer to $750 or closer to $1,000. Cuyahoga Metropolitan Housing Authority (CMHA) sets voucher payment standards by bedroom size and adjusts them periodically, so check CMHA's current chart rather than assuming a flat number.
