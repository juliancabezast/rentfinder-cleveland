2 Bedroom houses for rent in Cleveland under $900

Two-bedroom houses renting for under $900 a month are common on Cleveland's east and southeast side, in neighborhoods like Slavic Village, Collinwood, and Glenville. Rent Finder Cleveland's 2-bedroom rental homes generally start around $750 a month, and every home we manage accepts Section 8 Housing Choice Vouchers.

Updated July 4, 2026 ·4 min read ·By the Rent Finder Cleveland team

Are there 2-bedroom houses for rent in Cleveland under $900?

Yes. Within our own portfolio, 2-bedroom rental homes generally price between about $750 and $1,100 a month, and a meaningful share come in at or below $900 depending on the neighborhood, condition, and whether utilities are included. Two-bedroom units are also the single largest category in our Cleveland portfolio, so this price band has more options to choose from than 3- or 4-bedroom homes.

For context, Zumper's July 4, 2026 report puts Cleveland's citywide median 2-bedroom asking rent at $1,100 — meaning a sub-$900 2-bedroom house sits comfortably on the affordable end of the broader market, not just our own listings.

Which Cleveland neighborhoods have the most affordable 2-bedroom houses?

Our lowest-priced 2-bedroom inventory tends to cluster in the same east and southeast side neighborhoods where we manage the bulk of our homes overall. These areas also happen to be some of Cleveland's larger, more established residential districts, which keeps the supply of older, smaller rental houses relatively steady.

  • Slavic Village (ZIP 44105) — Cleveland's largest concentration of our rental homes, southeast of downtown along the Morgana Run Trail
  • Collinwood (ZIP 44110) — far northeast side, near the North Shore Collinwood lakefront area
  • Glenville (ZIP 44108) — northeast side, near University Circle and St. Clair Avenue
  • Buckeye-Shaker (ZIP 44120) — southeast side, along the RTA Blue/Green light-rail corridor

What does a 2-bedroom house near this price point typically include?

Most of the housing stock in Cleveland's east-side neighborhoods dates to the early 1900s: two-bedroom, one-bath homes and duplex units with front porches, small yards, and full basements. Amenities and finishes vary by property, so we only advertise specific features — updated kitchens, in-unit laundry, off-street parking — when a listing's own data confirms them, rather than promising them across the board.

Roughly half of our overall Cleveland portfolio is made up of duplexes rather than fully detached single-family houses, so a 2-bedroom "house" search at this price point often turns up one unit of a duplex or a triplex alongside standalone houses. We describe each listing's actual structure type rather than defaulting to "house" for every property.

Section 8 and vouchers for 2-bedroom houses under $900

Every 2-bedroom home in our Cleveland portfolio accepts Housing Choice Vouchers (Section 8) and is HUD-inspection-ready — this price band is where many voucher holders find a workable match, since payment standards are set relative to a unit's bedroom count and rent. Cuyahoga Metropolitan Housing Authority (CMHA) administers the local voucher program and publishes its own payment-standard figures; check the current chart directly with CMHA before assuming what a voucher will cover.
